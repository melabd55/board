ZIMMERMAN Ch 69 — Acute Rehabilitation & Early Mobility — EXAM MODE

Block A — Mock RCPSC SAQ (10 questions, 12 minutes)

A1. List the four primary disciplines that comprise the PICU rehabilitation team and one core responsibility of each.

Show answer
  • Physical therapist — movement, strength, gait, mobility progression.
  • Occupational therapist — ADLs, positioning, splinting, oral motor stimulation.
  • Speech-language pathologist — swallow safety, speech, augmentative communication.
  • Physiatrist — physician oversight of integrated rehab plan.

A2. Explain the rationale and timing for rehab consultation in the PICU.

Show answer
  • Consult within 72 hours of admission, regardless of acuity.
  • Rationale: harms of bed rest exceed risks of mobilization for most critically ill children.
  • 20% of children admitted > 72 h are completely immobile (Choong 2014).
  • Early mobility reduces delirium, ICU-AW, ventilator days, and PICS-p.

A3. Name and explain the ABCDEF bundle.

Show answer
  • A — Assess, prevent, and manage pain (FLACC, COMFORT-B, Wong-Baker).
  • B — Both spontaneous awakening and breathing trials daily.
  • C — Choice of analgesia and sedation (dexmedetomidine preferred over benzodiazepines).
  • D — Delirium screening and management (CAPD ≥ 9 positive).
  • E — Early mobility (7-level progression ladder).
  • F — Family engagement and empowerment.
  • Implementation reduces delirium, ventilator days, and mortality.

A4. Discuss the RESTORE trial findings and clinical implications.

Show answer
  • Curley JAMA 2015 — pediatric RCT of targeted sedation with daily arousal vs usual care in mechanically ventilated children.
  • No significant reduction in ventilator duration (primary outcome).
  • Significant reduction in opioid and benzodiazepine exposure.
  • No increase in unplanned extubation or line removal.
  • Demonstrates safety and feasibility of pediatric goal-directed sedation; foundational for PICU implementation of bundle.

A5. Define PICS-p and list its five domains.

Show answer
  • Post-Intensive Care Syndrome — Pediatric (Manning 2018).
  • Physical: weakness, fatigue, deconditioning.
  • Cognitive: attention, memory, executive function.
  • Emotional/psychological: anxiety, depression, PTSD.
  • Social: school re-entry, peer relationships.
  • Family: caregiver mental health (PICS-Family).

A6. A 2-month-old recovering from post-cardiac surgery prolonged ICU stay. Outline neurodevelopmentally supportive interventions.

Show answer
  • OT for oral motor stimulation to prevent oral aversion and shorten transition to oral feeding.
  • In-utero physiologic flexion positioning; hands toward face; midline head.
  • Rotate supine/side/prone positions to prevent plagiocephaly.
  • Kangaroo care (skin-to-skin) ≥ 1 h/day when stable — reduces stress hormones, improves long-term cognition (Charpak 2017).
  • Minimize benzodiazepines; consider dexmedetomidine.
  • Synactive Theory framework — support autonomic, motor, state regulation, attention, self-regulation.

A7. List the safety criteria for initiating active mobility in a critically ill child.

Show answer
  • Hemodynamic stability — no new vasopressor in past 1 hour.
  • Respiratory stability — FiO₂ ≤ 0.6, PEEP ≤ 10 cm H₂O.
  • Neurologic stability — no active ICP crisis.
  • No active arrhythmia.
  • No actively bleeding line or unstable surgical site.
  • Adequate sedation depth for cooperation when active mobility planned.

A8. CAPD cut-off and operating characteristics.

Show answer
  • Cornell Assessment of Pediatric Delirium; 8 items, score 0–21.
  • Score ≥ 9 = positive delirium screen.
  • Sensitivity ~94%, specificity ~79% (Traube 2014).
  • Validated across all pediatric ages including non-verbal and intubated.
  • Score every 12 hours minimum during PICU stay (SCCM 2022 guideline).

A9. A 10-year-old has been mechanically ventilated for 14 days. Family asks about long-term outcomes. Explain PICS-p framework and what to monitor.

Show answer
  • Explain PICS-p across all five domains.
  • Post-discharge follow-up plan:
    • Functional assessment with FSS, WeeFIM, 6MWT, PEDI-CAT at 3, 6, 12 months.
    • Neuropsychology assessment for cognitive concerns.
    • Mental health screen for anxiety, depression, PTSD.
    • School re-entry plan with IEP if needed.
    • Family mental health (PICS-Family) — screening and resources for caregivers.
  • Multidisciplinary post-PICU follow-up program when available.

A10. Discuss the role of the Passy-Muir valve.

Show answer
  • One-way speaking valve for tracheostomized patients with cuff deflated.
  • Directs exhaled air through vocal cords → restores phonation.
  • Improves communication, swallow function, and respiratory mechanics in select patients.
  • Requires SLP supervision and assessment of tolerance.
  • Contraindications: cuff cannot be deflated, severe airway obstruction, hemodynamic instability.

Block B — Oral Cases (5 sequential, 35 minutes)

B1. A 7-year-old previously well girl is on day 8 of ventilation for ARDS, deeply sedated on midazolam + fentanyl infusions, no paralytics on board. Family is asking when she can start to "wake up and move."

B1a — Initial rehab plan?
  • Consult PT, OT, SLP, physiatrist within 72 hours of admission (overdue here).
  • Daily arousal assessment; target sedation depth SBS –1 to 0.
  • Transition midazolam to dexmedetomidine where feasible to reduce delirium risk.
  • Initiate passive ROM and splinting at Level 1 of mobility ladder.
  • HOB 30° for VAP prevention.
  • Daily CAPD delirium screening.
B1b — As sedation lightens, she becomes agitated and pulls at lines. CAPD 12. Manage.
  • Delirium positive — multimodal approach.
  • Minimize benzodiazepines; continue dexmedetomidine.
  • Optimize sleep (clustered care, day-night cues).
  • Family presence at bedside.
  • Treat underlying triggers (pain, infection, withdrawal, electrolyte).
  • Consider quetiapine or risperidone for severe persistent agitation.
  • Continue early mobility (engagement, play, distraction).
B1c — Ready for advancement to active mobility. How to assess safety and progress?
  • Apply early mobility safety checklist.
  • If criteria met: progress to Level 3 (edge-of-bed sitting) with PT supervision.
  • Bedside RN, RT, PT all present for transfer.
  • Goal: advance one level per day as tolerated.
  • Document mobility goal at multidisciplinary rounds.
B1d — Day 18, extubated, transferring to ward. Discharge rehab plan.
  • Functional Status Scale and WeeFIM assessment.
  • Continued PT/OT/SLP on ward.
  • Physiatry follow-up.
  • PICS-p framework counseling for family.
  • School re-entry plan; outpatient rehab referral.
  • Mental health and family wellbeing assessment.

B2. A 3-month-old post-cardiac surgery infant has been in CICU for 21 days, still requiring low-flow oxygen. Family worried about developmental delay.

B2a — Neurodevelopmental rehab approach.
  • Synactive Theory framework — support autonomic, motor, state regulation.
  • OT for oral motor stimulation to prevent oral aversion.
  • Positioning: in-utero flexion, hands toward face, midline head; rotate frequently.
  • Kangaroo care ≥ 1 h/day.
  • Minimize sedation; light cycle promotion.
  • Cluster nursing care to allow sleep.
B2b — Family asks specifically about kangaroo care benefits.
  • Skin-to-skin contact with parent.
  • Reduces stress hormones (cortisol) in infant and parent.
  • Increases oxytocin.
  • Improves parent-child bonding.
  • Long-term cognitive and social benefits (Charpak 2017 — 20-year follow-up showing benefit to early adulthood).
  • Recommended ≥ 1 hour/day when medically feasible.
B2c — Plagiocephaly developing despite repositioning.
  • Aggressive repositioning protocol every 2 hours.
  • Increased tummy time when stable.
  • Avoid prolonged supine in same position.
  • Cranial molding helmet if persistent or severe (after age 4–6 months).
  • OT/PT for neck stretching if torticollis present.
B2d — Discharge planning.
  • Early Intervention referral.
  • Developmental pediatric follow-up.
  • Cardiac neurodevelopmental program (high-risk population).
  • Audiology and vision baseline.
  • Bayley assessment at 6, 12, 24 months.
  • PICS-Family resources.

B3. A 13-year-old after status asthmaticus with prolonged paralysis on rocuronium (day 9 paralysis). Now off paralytics but very weak.

B3a — Diagnosis and approach.
  • ICU-acquired weakness (ICU-AW) — multifactorial myopathy/neuropathy.
  • Risk: prolonged NMB, steroids, immobility.
  • Aggressive PT/OT — passive then active ROM.
  • Optimize nutrition (high protein) and glycemic control.
  • Minimize ongoing sedation.
  • Anticipate inpatient rehab placement.
  • Family PICS-p counseling.
B3b — Continued challenges with respiratory weakness.
  • Wean ventilator gradually with PT-supported respiratory training.
  • Inspiratory muscle training.
  • Lung volume recruitment.
  • If failure to wean, consider tracheostomy after multidisciplinary discussion.
  • Sleep optimization to reduce sympathetic overdrive.
B3c — Patient cognitively intact but unable to speak due to ETT.
  • SLP consult for augmentative communication.
  • Communication board, eye-gaze system, tablet apps.
  • If tracheostomy: trial Passy-Muir valve under SLP supervision.
  • Family engaged in interpreting nonverbal cues.
B3d — Long-term outcome expectations.
  • ICU-AW survivors often require inpatient rehab post-discharge.
  • Most recover muscle function over weeks to months with rehab.
  • Risk of persistent weakness, fatigue.
  • Cognitive and psychological sequelae possible.
  • School re-entry plan; outpatient PT/OT/SLP.
  • Follow-up FSS, WeeFIM, 6MWT.

B4. A 5-year-old after severe TBI, deeply sedated for ICP management on day 5.

B4a — Can early rehab be initiated even with severe TBI?
  • Yes — Level 1 passive interventions are appropriate even with deep sedation.
  • Passive ROM, splinting (anti-contracture), positioning, sensory stimulation.
  • OT for splinting to prevent fixed contractures.
  • Sensory stimulation may be neurologically beneficial.
  • HOB 30° aligned with ICP management.
B4b — Day 8, ICP improving; sedation weaning. Plan?
  • Daily arousal assessment.
  • cEEG to detect breakthrough seizures during sedation wean.
  • Advance to Level 2 (active-assisted ROM) as tolerated.
  • Cognitive-communication assessment by SLP as alertness returns.
  • Pain control to allow active participation.
B4c — Day 15, awake but emerging behavioral problems.
  • Post-traumatic agitation common in pediatric TBI recovery.
  • Minimize stimulation when overwhelmed.
  • Quiet environment; family presence; routine.
  • Avoid restraints; redirect rather than confront.
  • Neuropsychology consult.
  • Consider quetiapine or risperidone if severe.
B4d — Discharge planning to rehab facility.
  • Inpatient pediatric rehabilitation referral.
  • Comprehensive functional assessment (FSS, WeeFIM, GOS-E Peds).
  • Family training and education.
  • School re-entry plan with neuropsychological assessment.
  • Long-term outpatient PT/OT/SLP/neuropsych.
  • Mental health screening for family (PICS-Family).

B5. An 11-year-old with severe sepsis and refractory shock, paralyzed and sedated for 7 days, now stabilizing.

B5a — Rehab consult and early steps.
  • Rehab consult immediately even while paralyzed.
  • OT/PT for passive ROM, splinting.
  • Plan to wean paralysis ASAP — daily TOF assessment.
  • Reassess sedation depth daily once off paralytics.
  • CAPD delirium screen.
B5b — Significant ICU-AW once off paralytics.
  • Aggressive rehab — PT, OT, dietitian, physiatry.
  • Optimize nutrition (high protein, adequate calories).
  • Glycemic control.
  • Minimize ongoing sedation.
  • Plan for inpatient rehab post-PICU.
B5c — Sleep disruption is severe.
  • Clustered care for uninterrupted sleep blocks.
  • Dimmed lights at night, daylight exposure during day.
  • Minimize alarm noise.
  • Avoid late-evening sedative interruptions.
  • Family presence.
  • Melatonin in selected children.
B5d — Family expressing high anxiety about long-term outcome.
  • Honest PICS-p education.
  • Engage social work, psychology, chaplaincy.
  • Realistic outcome expectations.
  • PICS-Family resources.
  • Post-PICU follow-up program enrollment.
  • Mental health screening for family.

Block C — Randomized SAQ Drill (20)

C1. When to consult rehab team in PICU.

Show
  • Physical, cognitive, emotional, social, family.

C3. CAPD positive cut-off.

Show
  • Score ≥ 9 of 21 = positive delirium screen.

C4. RESTORE trial — key result.

Show
  • Targeted sedation reduced opioid/BZD exposure; no harm; no increase in unplanned extubation.

C5. VAP prevention positioning.

Show

C6. ABCDEF bundle elements.

Show
  • Assess pain; Both awakening + breathing trials; Choice of sedation; Delirium; Early mobility; Family.

C7. Optimal positioning for infant < 3 months in PICU.

Show
  • Physiologic (in-utero) flexion; hands toward face; midline head; rotate positions.

C8. Kangaroo care duration target.

Show
  • ≥ 1 hour/day when medically feasible.

C9. Passy-Muir valve indication.

Show
  • Tracheostomized patient with cuff deflated, to restore phonation.

C10. Mobility safety criteria.

Show
  • Hemodynamic stability, FiO₂ ≤ 0.6, PEEP ≤ 10, no ICP crisis, no arrhythmia, no bleeding line.

C11. Sedation depth scales pediatric.

Show
  • SBS, RASS, COMFORT-B.

C12. Benzodiazepine vs delirium.

Show
  • BZD strongly associated with delirium; dexmedetomidine preferred where feasible.

C13. 7-level mobility ladder — Level 4.

Show
  • Bed-to-chair transfer, with or without ventilator.

C14. Why oral motor stimulation in infants.

Show
  • Prevent oral aversion after prolonged intubation; shorten transition to oral feeding.

C15. Functional outcome measure 5–13 y.

Show
  • Timed Up and Go (TUG); 30-second walk test; 6-minute walk test.

C16. ICU-AW risk factors.

Show
  • Prolonged paralysis, steroids, immobility, sepsis, hyperglycemia.

C17. Sleep promotion strategies.

Show
  • Cluster care, day-night light cues, minimize alarms, family presence, melatonin select cases.

C18. Sarnel Synactive Theory components.

Show
  • Autonomic, motor, state regulation, attention/interaction, self-regulation.

C19. Augmentative communication examples.

Show
  • Passy-Muir valve, communication board, eye-gaze system, tablet apps (Proloquo2Go).

C20. PICS-Family component.

Show
  • Caregiver mental health screening — anxiety, depression, PTSD; resources and support.
