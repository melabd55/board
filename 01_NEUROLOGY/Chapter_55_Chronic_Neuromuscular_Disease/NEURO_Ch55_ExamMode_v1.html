Mock RCPSC Block A — 10 SAQs (timed)

⏱ 30 minutes total — 3 min per item
SAQ A-1
Define the three "outlier phenotypes" suggesting underlying chronic NMD in the PICU.
Show Answer
  • Neonatal respiratory distress in a non-preterm infant without obvious lung disease.
  • Later-onset isolated diaphragmatic weakness (think Pompe, late-onset SMA, MuSK-MG).
  • Bulbar dysfunction — recurrent aspiration, dysphagia, dysarthria — without anatomic cause.
SAQ A-2
Outline the respiratory trajectory of DMD and key intervention thresholds.
Show Answer
  • FVC peaks at ~age 10, declines ~5 % per year.
  • Nocturnal NIV when FVC <50 % predicted or nocturnal hypoventilation on sleep study.
  • Daytime NIV when FVC <30 %.
  • Tracheostomy when FVC <10 %, bulbar weakness, or recurrent NIV failure.
  • Steroids and exon-skipping / gene therapy flatten the slope.
SAQ A-3
Why is supplemental oxygen alone hazardous in advanced DMD respiratory failure?
Show Answer
  • Primary problem is hypoventilation with hypercapnia, not hypoxia.
  • O₂ corrects SpO₂ but masks rising PaCO₂.
  • Risks acute hypercapnic acidosis and CO₂ narcosis.
  • Always start NIV first; titrate O₂ only after ventilation is supported.
SAQ A-4
List anesthesia precautions for RYR1-related congenital myopathy.
Show Answer
  • Avoid volatile anesthetics (sevoflurane, isoflurane, desflurane).
  • Avoid succinylcholine.
  • Use TIVA — propofol + remifentanil + non-depolarizing NMBA at reduced dose.
  • Have dantrolene available.
  • Continuous ETCO₂ and core temperature.
  • Clean / vapor-free anesthesia machine or activated charcoal filters.
SAQ A-5
Outline SMA modifying therapy options.
Show Answer
  • Nusinersen — intrathecal antisense oligonucleotide; 4 loading doses then every 4 months.
  • Onasemnogene abeparvovec — single IV AAV9 gene therapy; approved <2 years.
  • Risdiplam — oral SMN2 splicing modifier; any age >2 months.
  • Earlier initiation = better motor outcome; pre-symptomatic via newborn screening best.
SAQ A-6
Cough peak flow thresholds and action.
Show Answer
  • >270 L/min — normal.
  • 160–270 — borderline; use proactive techniques during illness.
  • <160 — initiate MI-E (mechanical insufflation-exsufflation) routinely.
  • High risk of mucus plugging during respiratory infection.
SAQ A-7
When is gastrostomy indicated in chronic NMD?
Show Answer
  • Aspiration on swallow study.
  • Growth failure or inadequate oral intake.
  • Feeds taking >30–45 minutes.
  • Frequent meal-related desaturation.
  • Best placed while FVC >50 % to minimize anesthetic risk.
  • Consider fundoplication if severe GERD.
SAQ A-8
Cardiac surveillance in DMD.
Show Answer
  • Annual ECG from diagnosis.
  • Annual echo + cardiac MRI from age 10 (earlier if abnormal).
  • Start ACE-inhibitor ± carvedilol at age 10 regardless of LV function.
  • Intensify with eplerenone / sacubitril-valsartan if dysfunction develops.
  • Discuss ICD if guideline indications met.
SAQ A-9
What is riboflavin transporter deficiency and why does it matter?
Show Answer
  • SLC52A2/A3 mutations → Brown-Vialetto-Van Laere syndrome.
  • Presents with bulbar weakness, sensorineural deafness, motor neuron–like features.
  • Treatable with high-dose riboflavin 10–80 mg/kg/day.
  • Critical treatable mimic of bulbar SMA / motor neuron disease — early initiation matters.
SAQ A-10
List CMS subtypes and their therapy direction.
Show Answer
  • Presynaptic ChAT — pyridostigmine + manage episodic apnea.
  • Synaptic COLQ — anticholinesterases contraindicated; salbutamol / ephedrine.
  • Postsynaptic AChR-ε / RAPSN / fast-channel — pyridostigmine ± 3,4-DAP.
  • DOK7 — salbutamol / ephedrine; anticholinesterases worsen.
  • Slow-channel — fluoxetine / quinidine; anticholinesterases worsen.

Mock RCPSC Block B — 5 Sequential ICU Cases

⏱ 30 minutes total — 6 min per case
CASE B-1
3-week-old term infant with severe hypotonia, areflexia, tongue fasciculations, intercostal recession; SMN1 deletion confirmed.
Q. Immediate management.
Show Answer
  • Diagnosis: SMA type 1.
  • Supportive ventilation (NIV initially; intubation if airway failure).
  • Urgent goals-of-care discussion including modifying therapy.
  • Options: onasemnogene (<2 y, anti-AAV9 titer permitting, prednisolone pre-treat) or nusinersen + risdiplam.
  • Multidisciplinary team — neurology, palliative care, social work, dietitian.
  • Family genetic counselling.
CASE B-2
12-year-old DMD on deflazacort admitted with influenza A; hypoxia, hypercapnia; home NIV user.
Q. Manage.
Show Answer
  • Continue NIV; do not give supplemental O₂ alone.
  • Oseltamivir, MI-E, chest physiotherapy.
  • Broad-spectrum antibiotics if bacterial superinfection suspected.
  • Continue deflazacort; add stress-dose hydrocortisone for systemic illness.
  • Echo and ECG to assess cardiac function.
  • Multidisciplinary discussion of escalation ceiling and palliative care involvement.
CASE B-3
7-year-old boy with newly diagnosed DMD presents for elective ear-tube insertion under GA.
Q. Anesthetic plan.
Show Answer
  • TIVA — propofol + remifentanil + non-depolarizing NMBA at reduced dose.
  • Avoid volatile anesthetics; avoid succinylcholine (hyperkalemia + MH risk).
  • ETCO₂ + core temperature monitoring; dantrolene available.
  • Pre-op ECG and echo if not recent.
  • Post-op respiratory monitoring; have NIV ready.
CASE B-4
17-year-old with newly diagnosed isolated diaphragmatic weakness, FVC 38 %, no limb weakness, mild proximal weakness.
Q. Workup and treatment.
Show Answer
  • Suspect Pompe disease (late-onset / LOPD).
  • GAA enzyme assay (dried blood spot or leukocyte), urine glucose tetrasaccharide.
  • GAA gene confirmation.
  • Initiate enzyme replacement (alglucosidase alfa or avalglucosidase alfa).
  • NIV bridge.
  • Cardiology and respiratory follow-up.
CASE B-5
DMD adolescent post spinal fusion fails extubation twice; pre-op FVC 35 %.
Q. Strategy.
Show Answer
  • Plan extubation directly to NIV (do not extubate to room air).
  • Optimize MI-E and chest physiotherapy pre-extubation.
  • Minimize opioids; consider regional analgesia (epidural / intercostal blocks).
  • Treat any infection / atelectasis.
  • If recurrent failure, discuss tracheostomy with family.
  • Palliative care integration alongside active treatment.

Block C — Remaining SAQs (randomized)

Q C-1
List SMA types and their motor milestones.
Show Answer
  • Type 0 — prenatal/birth; no motor milestones; severe.
  • Type 1 (Werdnig-Hoffmann) — <6 mo; never sits.
  • Type 2 — 6–18 mo; sits, never walks.
  • Type 3 (Kugelberg-Welander) — >18 mo; walks, may lose ambulation.
  • Type 4 — adult onset; ambulatory.
Q C-2
Describe nusinersen mechanism and dosing.
Show Answer
  • Antisense oligonucleotide modifying SMN2 splicing to include exon 7.
  • Increases functional SMN protein in motor neurons.
  • Intrathecal 12 mg — loading doses 0, 14, 28, 63 days, then every 4 months.
Q C-3
Onasemnogene mechanism and adverse events.
Show Answer
  • Single IV AAV9-SMN1 gene therapy delivering functional SMN1.
  • Approved <2 years of age.
  • Adverse events: transaminitis, thrombocytopenia, troponin elevation, rare thrombotic microangiopathy.
  • Prophylactic prednisolone 1 mg/kg/d starting day −1 with taper based on LFTs.
Q C-4
Describe congenital myotonic dystrophy.
Show Answer
  • DM1 with very large CTG repeat (>1000), maternally inherited.
  • Floppy neonate, polyhydramnios, club feet, feeding difficulty, respiratory failure.
  • Mother often has only mild grip myotonia — examine her.
  • Cardiac conduction defects develop later.
  • May improve in childhood then progress.
Q C-5
DM1 cardiac conduction implications.
Show Answer
  • Progressive AV block (1st → 2nd → BBB → complete heart block).
  • Sudden cardiac death risk even with normal LV function.
  • Annual ECG mandatory.
  • Pacemaker / ICD per criteria.
  • Avoid QT-prolonging drugs.
Q C-6
Congenital muscular dystrophy subtypes.
Show Answer
  • Merosin-deficient (LAMA2) — white matter signal on MRI, normal cognition.
  • Ullrich (collagen VI, COL6A1/2/3) — distal hyperlaxity + proximal contractures.
  • Walker-Warburg (POMT1) — brain malformations, severe respiratory weakness.
  • Fukuyama (FKTN) — brain malformations, common in Japan.
Q C-7
Congenital myopathy subtypes.
Show Answer
  • Nemaline — rod bodies; NEB, ACTA1.
  • Centronuclear / myotubular — central nuclei; MTM1, DNM2, BIN1.
  • Multi-minicore — multiple small cores; SEPN1, RYR1.
  • Central core — single core; RYR1.
Q C-8
Role of NIV during acute exacerbation.
Show Answer
  • First-line for hypoventilation; continue or initiate during acute exacerbation.
  • Use AVAPS or volume-controlled mode for fluctuating compliance.
  • Add MI-E for secretions.
  • Avoid supplemental O₂ alone.
  • Failure (persistent acidosis, bulbar collapse, mental status decline) → consider intubation with extubation plan.
Q C-9
Palliative care integration in chronic NMD.
Show Answer
  • Integrate from first PICU admission, not at end-of-life.
  • Clarify ceilings of care and advance directives.
  • Symptom management — dyspnea, pain, anxiety.
  • Family support and respite planning.
  • School re-integration and transition planning.
  • After-death follow-up.
Q C-10
Scoliosis surgery timing in DMD.
Show Answer
  • Cobb angle 20–30° in DMD — operate early.
  • Ideally before FVC falls below 30–40 % predicted.
  • Late surgery carries higher pulmonary, cardiac, anesthetic risk.
  • Pre-op: cardiology clearance, NIV training, MH precautions, multidisciplinary planning.
Q C-11
Multidisciplinary chronic NMD care plan elements.
Show Answer
  • Neuromuscular neurology.
  • Pulmonology + sleep medicine + RT + MI-E.
  • Cardiology.
  • Orthopedic surgery (scoliosis, contractures).
  • Physical / occupational therapy.
  • Speech-language pathology + GI + nutrition.
  • Medical genetics + family counselling.
  • Mental health + social work + school services.
  • Palliative care + case manager.
Q C-12
Floppy newborn diagnostic workup.
Show Answer
  • History — pregnancy (polyhydramnios), family history of NMD.
  • Exam — tone, reflexes, antigravity movement, dysmorphism, mother's grip myotonia.
  • SMN1 deletion + SMN2 copy number.
  • Congenital myopathy / muscular dystrophy panel.
  • DM1 CTG repeat in mother.
  • CMS panel; mitochondrial / metabolic screen.
  • Brain MRI if dysmorphism / seizures.
  • Echo if cardiac suspicion.
Q C-13
CMT type 3 (Dejerine-Sottas) neonatal implications.
Show Answer
  • Severe congenital hypomyelinating neuropathy.
  • Hypotonia, areflexia, very low motor/sensory conduction (often <5 m/s).
  • Some require ventilator support.
  • PMP22 / MPZ / EGR2.
  • No disease-modifying therapy; supportive care and rehab.
Q C-14
Vaccines and prophylaxis specific to chronic NMD.
Show Answer
  • Annual influenza.
  • COVID-19 per local schedule.
  • PCV20 (or PCV13 + PPSV23).
  • VZV, hepatitis B series.
  • RSV monoclonal (nirsevimab) for eligible infants/toddlers; palivizumab historically.
  • Avoid live vaccines on high-dose steroids.
  • Household contacts vaccinated.
Q C-15
Cardiac involvement across pediatric NMD.
Show Answer
  • DMD/BMD — dilated cardiomyopathy + conduction defects.
  • Myotonic dystrophy — progressive conduction disease, SCD risk.
  • Pompe (infantile) — hypertrophic cardiomyopathy.
  • Friedreich ataxia — hypertrophic cardiomyopathy.
  • Carnitine deficiency — dilated cardiomyopathy.
  • Congenital myopathies — usually spared (centronuclear sometimes affected).
Q C-16
Role of steroids in DMD.
Show Answer
  • Deflazacort 0.9 mg/kg/d or prednisone 0.75 mg/kg/d.
  • Start age 4–6 regardless of ambulation status.
  • Delays loss of ambulation by 2–3 years.
  • Slows respiratory decline; may delay cardiomyopathy.
  • Side effects — weight gain, growth restriction, osteoporosis, cataracts.
  • Never stop abruptly during acute illness.
Q C-17
Ataluren and exon-skipping in DMD.
Show Answer
  • Ataluren — read-through agent for nonsense (stop-codon) mutations.
  • Eteplirsen (exon 51), golodirsen (exon 53), casimersen (exon 45) — exon-skipping antisense oligonucleotides.
  • Restore reading frame in eligible mutations.
  • Modest dystrophin restoration; benefit debated; approved in some jurisdictions.
Q C-18
Delandistrogene moxeparvovec indications.
Show Answer
  • AAV-microdystrophin gene therapy.
  • FDA approved 2023 for ambulatory DMD 4–7 y; expanded 2024.
  • Single IV infusion.
  • Watch for hepatotoxicity, myocarditis, myositis.
  • Long-term efficacy still emerging.
Q C-19
When is tracheostomy considered in chronic NMD?
Show Answer
  • NIV inadequate.
  • >16 h/day ventilation required.
  • Bulbar weakness preventing NIV mask seal or with recurrent aspiration.
  • Recurrent NIV failure.
  • Decision involves patient, family, multidisciplinary team, palliative care.
  • Plan home ventilation, trained caregivers, equipment, emergency protocols.
Q C-20
Long-term ventilation ethical issues.
Show Answer
  • Adolescent assent vs. parent decision-making.
  • Quality of life judgments.
  • Resource allocation.
  • Reversibility considerations.
  • Advance care planning.
  • Withdrawal vs. withholding.
  • Family caregiver burden.
Q C-21
Pompe disease enzyme replacement details.
Show Answer
  • Alglucosidase alfa or avalglucosidase alfa.
  • Initiate early in infantile-onset to prolong survival.
  • CRIM-negative patients need immune-tolerance induction.
  • Monitor for infusion-related reactions.
  • Pre-medicate (antihistamine + steroid + slow rate) if hypersensitivity.
Q C-22
Mitochondrial disease anesthetic considerations.
Show Answer
  • Avoid prolonged fasting; give 10 % dextrose.
  • Avoid propofol infusion >1 h (PRIS risk).
  • Avoid lactate-containing fluids where possible.
  • Limit volatile anesthetics; use short-acting agents.
  • Slower recovery and post-op respiratory monitoring.
Q C-23
Friedreich ataxia features relevant to PICU.
Show Answer
  • Progressive sensory ataxia.
  • Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy — leading cause of death.
  • Diabetes, kyphoscoliosis, sensorineural deafness, optic atrophy.
  • Admissions usually for cardiac decompensation or scoliosis surgery.
  • MH-safe (not RYR1-mediated); avoid hypotension.
Q C-24
SMA newborn screening rationale.
Show Answer
  • Identify pre-symptomatic SMA for early disease-modifying therapy.
  • Onasemnogene preferred <2 months in infants with 1–3 SMN2 copies.
  • Pre-symptomatic treatment produces near-normal motor outcomes.
  • Symptomatic treatment much less effective.
  • Universal screening since 2018 (US RUSP).
Q C-25
Differentiate myasthenic crisis from acute exacerbation in chronic CMS.
Show Answer
  • Autoimmune MG — Ab-mediated, responds to IVIG/PLEX and immunotherapy.
  • CMS — genetic, requires subtype identification.
  • Some CMS subtypes (slow-channel, DOK7, COLQ) worsen with anticholinesterases.
  • SFEMG and genetic testing inform subtype.
  • Triggers — infection, drugs, stress, missed doses.
Q C-26
NIV initiation in pediatric NMD — steps.
Show Answer
  • Confirm indication (sleep study, daytime CO₂, FVC, symptoms).
  • Choose interface — nasal mask preferred; full-face for mouth breathers.
  • Mode — BiPAP S/T or AVAPS.
  • Initial settings — IPAP 8–10 over EPAP 4–5 with backup rate; titrate to relieve WOB and normalize CO₂.
  • Acclimatize awake first.
  • Re-evaluate every 6 months with sleep study.
Q C-27
Limb-girdle muscular dystrophy ICU considerations.
Show Answer
  • Subtype-dependent — variable cardiomyopathy and respiratory involvement.
  • Calpainopathy (R1/LGMD2A) — rhabdomyolysis risk with eccentric exercise.
  • Sarcoglycanopathies, FKRP — cardiomyopathy possible.
  • Pre-anesthesia: ECG + echo; MH precautions until subtype confirmed safe; reduced NMBA.
Q C-28
Bone health in long-term DMD.
Show Answer
  • Steroid-induced osteoporosis.
  • Vertebral fractures common — even from minor trauma.
  • Calcium + vitamin D optimization.
  • DEXA monitoring.
  • Bisphosphonate (zoledronate, pamidronate) for symptomatic fracture or low BMD.
  • Continue steroids — abrupt withdrawal worsens muscle function.
Q C-29
Adolescent transition-of-care planning.
Show Answer
  • Begin at 14–16 y.
  • Multidisciplinary transition clinic.
  • Identify adult neuromuscular neurologist + pulmonologist + cardiologist.
  • Patient education for self-management.
  • Family role redefinition.
  • Insurance / disability paperwork.
  • Educational and vocational planning.
Q C-30
MH precautions essentials.
Show Answer
  • Avoid volatile anesthetics and succinylcholine.
  • Use TIVA with non-depolarizing NMBA.
  • Dantrolene available; ETCO₂ and core temperature monitored.
  • Clean / vapor-free anesthesia machine or activated charcoal filters.
  • Continuous post-op monitoring 24 h.

Block D — Remaining Case Bank (randomized)

CASE D-1
9-month-old floppy infant, SMN1 deletion confirmed, 2 SMN2 copies.
Q. Therapy?
Show Answer
  • SMA 1 with 2 SMN2 copies — severe phenotype.
  • Onasemnogene if <2 y and anti-AAV9 titer permits.
  • Alternative — nusinersen intrathecal + risdiplam oral combination.
  • Pre-treat prednisolone for onasemnogene.
  • Family counselling on prognosis.
CASE D-2
14-year-old DMD; FVC 28 %, morning headache, somnolence; sleep study shows REM hypoventilation.
Q. Next step.
Show Answer
  • Initiate nocturnal NIV (BiPAP S/T, IPAP 12 over EPAP 4, backup rate).
  • Plan daytime NIV when FVC <30 %.
  • Continue steroids and cardiac protection.
CASE D-3
3-month-old IUGR infant with tachypnea, paradoxical breathing, right hemidiaphragm elevation.
Q. Diagnosis and confirmation.
Show Answer
  • SMARD1 (IGHMBP2 mutation).
  • Confirm with IGHMBP2 genetic testing.
  • Long-term ventilator support required.
  • Distinguish from diaphragmatic eventration — SMARD1 progresses.
CASE D-4
2-year-old congenital myotonic dystrophy admitted with pneumonia.
Q. ICU and anesthetic concerns.
Show Answer
  • Prolonged NMBA effect — reduce dose.
  • Conduction defects — ECG, consider continuous monitoring.
  • Avoid suxamethonium (myotonic crisis).
  • Avoid cold / shivering.
  • Opioid bolus risk of apnea.
  • Mother often has subclinical DM1 — implications for parental care planning.
CASE D-5
6-month-old with central core disease undergoes emergency appendectomy.
Q. Anesthesia.
Show Answer
  • MH precautions — TIVA, no volatile, no succinylcholine.
  • Dantrolene available.
  • Continuous ETCO₂ + core temperature.
  • Clean anesthesia machine or charcoal filters.
CASE D-6
11-year-old DMD with new symptomatic dilated cardiomyopathy (EF 38 %).
Q. Management.
Show Answer
  • Initiate / intensify ACE-I + carvedilol.
  • Add eplerenone / mineralocorticoid antagonist.
  • Consider sacubitril/valsartan in older adolescents.
  • Continue steroids.
  • Annual MRI.
  • ICD discussion per criteria; heart transplant in selected refractory cases.
CASE D-7
5-year-old with merosin-deficient CMD for elective gastrostomy. MRI brain has white matter signal.
Q. Implication.
Show Answer
  • Merosin (laminin α2) deficiency — white matter signal is characteristic.
  • Does NOT indicate cognitive impairment.
  • Plan TIVA; careful airway; post-op NIV ready.
CASE D-8
2-year-old with severe weakness, deafness, bulbar dysfunction; MRI brainstem T2 hyperintensity.
Q. Treatable mimic.
Show Answer
  • Brown-Vialetto-Van Laere / riboflavin transporter deficiency (SLC52A2/A3).
  • High-dose riboflavin (10–80 mg/kg/d) can reverse or halt progression.
  • Early initiation matters; genetic confirmation in parallel.
CASE D-9
Adolescent DM1 for cholecystectomy.
Q. Risk profile.
Show Answer
  • ECG (conduction abnormalities — consider temp pacing).
  • Echo (cardiomyopathy).
  • Avoid suxamethonium.
  • Expect prolonged NMBA effect.
  • Post-op respiratory monitoring 24–48 h.
  • Avoid opioid bolus; maintain warmth.
CASE D-10
12-year-old with X-linked SMA (UBA1) and respiratory failure.
Q. Distinguishing features and management.
Show Answer
  • Rare X-linked variant; no SMN1-modifying therapy available.
  • Supportive ventilation; NIV first; tracheostomy if recurrent failure.
  • Family genetic counselling for X-linked inheritance.
CASE D-11
9-year-old congenital myopathy + scoliosis Cobb 55°, FVC 32 %.
Q. Surgery plan.
Show Answer
  • Cardiology clearance, NIV training pre-op.
  • MH precautions if RYR1.
  • Blood-loss preparation (tranexamic acid, cell saver).
  • Plan extubation directly to NIV.
  • Aggressive post-op physio and MI-E.
  • Discuss conservative options openly — late surgery with FVC <40 % is high-risk.
CASE D-12
CMS infant on pyridostigmine deteriorates after dose increase.
Q. Subtype, action.
Show Answer
  • Slow-channel CMS, COLQ, or DOK7 — anticholinesterases worsen these subtypes.
  • Stop pyridostigmine.
  • Switch — fluoxetine / quinidine (slow channel) or salbutamol / ephedrine (DOK7 / COLQ).
  • Confirm subtype by genetic panel.
CASE D-13
SMA1 toddler 5 days post-onasemnogene develops jaundice, AST/ALT 800.
Q. Action.
Show Answer
  • Onasemnogene-related transaminitis.
  • Increase prednisolone to 2 mg/kg/day.
  • Check coagulation; close monitoring.
  • Most respond to steroid escalation.
  • Continue SMA team follow-up.
CASE D-14
Adolescent DMD post-delandistrogene moxeparvovec with myocarditis-like presentation.
Q. Action.
Show Answer
  • Possible gene-therapy-associated myocarditis.
  • Echo, troponin, cardiac MRI.
  • Steroid therapy; supportive cardiac care.
  • Reported but rare; long-term implications evolving.
CASE D-15
Newborn with profound hypotonia and IUGR; mother has subclinical grip-myotonia.
Q. Diagnosis and family implication.
Show Answer
  • Congenital myotonic dystrophy (DM1) inherited from mother.
  • Very large CTG repeat (>1000).
  • Autosomal-dominant with anticipation.
  • Future pregnancies — prenatal genetic testing options.
CASE D-16
3-year-old SMA2 in PICU with severe pneumonia, baseline NIV.
Q. Approach.
Show Answer
  • Continue / expand NIV; MI-E; antibiotics; chest physio.
  • Consider IVIG if recurrent infections + low IgG.
  • Nutrition optimization.
  • Continue SMN-modifying therapy (nusinersen / risdiplam).
  • Multidisciplinary discussion of escalation ceiling.
CASE D-17
Adolescent with LGMD R1 (calpainopathy) and rhabdomyolysis after exercise.
Q. Management.
Show Answer
  • Aggressive hydration; urine output target.
  • Monitor K⁺, phosphate, calcium.
  • Treat AKI if develops.
  • CK trending.
  • Long-term — avoid intense eccentric exercise; cardiac surveillance per subtype.
CASE D-18
DMD considering scoliosis surgery at age 12, FVC 48 %.
Q. Timing rationale.
Show Answer
  • Surgery at FVC >30–40 % carries acceptable risk.
  • Earlier (Cobb 20–30°) recommended in DMD.
  • Cardiac clearance, NIV training, post-op NIV planning.
  • Multidisciplinary planning.
CASE D-19
Pompe infant (CRIM-negative) starting ERT.
Q. Special consideration.
Show Answer
  • Immune-tolerance induction (rituximab + methotrexate ± IVIG) before / at ERT start.
  • Prevents neutralizing antibodies that reduce efficacy.
  • CRIM status determines protocol intensity.
  • Cardiology and respiratory care concurrent.
CASE D-20
Floppy newborn; mother had polyhydramnios pregnancy.
Q. Top differential and bedside test.
Show Answer
  • Differentials — congenital DM1, SMA 0/1, severe congenital myopathy, CMD.
  • Bedside — examine mother for grip myotonia.
  • SMN1 deletion test.
  • Polyhydramnios reflects fetal swallow weakness.
CASE D-21
SMA3 ambulatory child loses ambulation at age 12 during viral illness.
Q. Plan.
Show Answer
  • Treat acute illness; intensive rehab.
  • Evaluate modifying therapy intensification (nusinersen increase, risdiplam add).
  • Plan ambulatory aids, school accommodations.
  • Cardiac and respiratory surveillance.
CASE D-22
DMD on home ventilation via tracheostomy with mucus plugging and desaturation.
Q. Bedside steps.
Show Answer
  • Suction first; check tube position.
  • Change tube if obstruction not relieved.
  • Manual bag-tube ventilation as needed.
  • MI-E.
  • Antibiotics if infection signs.
  • Optimize humidification.
CASE D-23
CMS infant on chronic 3,4-DAP develops seizure.
Q. Mechanism and action.
Show Answer
  • 3,4-DAP lowers seizure threshold.
  • Stop 3,4-DAP; treat with benzodiazepine.
  • Exclude metabolic seizure causes.
  • Restart 3,4-DAP at reduced dose if essential.
CASE D-24
Adolescent MELAS in status epilepticus.
Q. Priorities.
Show Answer
  • Avoid valproate (mitochondrial / hepatotoxic).
  • Avoid prolonged propofol (PRIS).
  • Levetiracetam first-line.
  • Maintain glucose; treat acidosis.
  • Cardiac monitoring (cardiomyopathy / conduction).
  • Arginine / CoQ10 / L-carnitine per protocol.
CASE D-25
CMT1A adolescent admitted post-trauma.
Q. Anesthetic concerns.
Show Answer
  • Generally MH-safe.
  • Avoid succinylcholine (hyperkalemia in chronic neuropathy).
  • Sensitivity to non-depolarizing NMBA — reduce dose.
  • Post-op chest physio if respiratory muscle involvement.
CASE D-26
DMD on long-term steroids has vertebral fracture from minor fall.
Q. Management.
Show Answer
  • Pain management without compromising respiratory drive.
  • Bracing as tolerated.
  • Calcium + vitamin D optimization.
  • DEXA monitoring.
  • Bisphosphonate (zoledronate or pamidronate) for symptomatic fracture.
  • Continue steroids — do not stop abruptly.
CASE D-27
Friedreich ataxia adolescent with VT.
Q. Management.
Show Answer
  • Treat VT (amiodarone; defibrillate if unstable).
  • Echo / MRI for hypertrophic cardiomyopathy.
  • ICD consideration.
  • Cardiology referral.
  • Avoid beta-blocker boluses that drop BP excessively.
CASE D-28
Centronuclear myopathy adolescent has prolonged apnea post-anesthesia.
Q. Future plan.
Show Answer
