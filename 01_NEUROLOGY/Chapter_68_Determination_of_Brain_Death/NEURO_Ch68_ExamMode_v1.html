Block A — Mock RCPSC SAQ (10 questions, 12 minutes)

A1. Define brain death and state its legal basis in most jurisdictions.

Show answer
  • Brain death = irreversible cessation of all functions of the entire brain, including the brainstem.
  • Legal basis: Uniform Determination of Death Act (UDDA, 1981) — death = irreversible cessation of (a) circulatory and respiratory function OR (b) all functions of entire brain including brainstem.
  • Pediatric criteria per Nakagawa 2011 (SCCM/AAP/CNS) and updated Greer 2023 unified consensus.

A2. List all prerequisites before initiating pediatric brain death determination.

Show answer
  • Known irreversible cause of coma (TBI, AHT, HIE, etc.).
  • Core temperature ≥ 36°C.
  • MAP age-appropriate (≥ 5th centile); shock corrected.
  • Severe metabolic/electrolyte/glucose derangements corrected.
  • Sedatives metabolized (4–5 half-lives; verify with drug levels when possible).
  • NMB reversed (TOF 4/4 on peripheral nerve stimulator).
  • Adequate observation period (typically ≥ 24 h after acute injury).

A3. State the pediatric apnea test threshold and abort criteria.

Show answer
  • Threshold: PaCO₂ ≥ 60 mm Hg AND ≥ 20 mm Hg above pre-test baseline.
  • Test positive if no respiratory effort throughout 8–10 minute observation.
  • Abort criteria:
    • SpO₂ < 85% sustained.
    • Significant hemodynamic instability.
    • Arrhythmia.
    • PaCO₂ target cannot be safely reached.
  • If aborted: reconnect ventilator; document; request ancillary study.

A4. A 3-week-old neonate after severe perinatal HIE meets brain death criteria on initial examination. State the observation interval and special exam components.

Show answer
  • Term newborn to 30 days: 24-hour observation interval between two examinations.
  • Each examination by a different qualified physician.
  • Each must include full clinical exam + apnea test.
  • Special components in neonates:
    • Test sucking and rooting reflexes (in addition to standard reflexes).
    • Apnea test challenges: pulmonary status, hemodynamic stability.
    • EEG less reliable due to inherently low-voltage neonatal background.
    • Cerebral perfusion scintigraphy preferred over EEG when ancillary needed.
  • Preterm < 37 wk: no formal Nakagawa 2011 criteria — case-by-case approach.

A5. List the brainstem reflexes that must be absent for brain death determination.

Show answer
  • Pupillary (midposition or dilated; unreactive to bright light).
  • Corneal (no blink to cotton or saline).
  • Oculocephalic ("doll's eyes" — avoid in c-spine instability).
  • Oculovestibular (cold caloric with 50 mL ice water in each ear, HOB 30°, ≥5 min between).
  • Gag (posterior pharynx stimulation).
  • Cough (deep endotracheal suctioning).
  • Sucking and rooting (in neonates and young infants).
  • Facial / jaw / grimace movements.
  • Respiratory effort (apnea test).

A6. A 9-year-old on VA-ECMO post-arrest is being considered for brain death determination. Discuss apnea testing modifications.

Show answer
  • VA-ECMO provides oxygenation and CO₂ removal regardless of native respiration — standard apnea test cannot be reliably performed.
  • Modification: reduce sweep gas flow to allow CO₂ accumulation.
  • Serial ABGs to track PaCO₂ rise.
  • Same threshold applies (PaCO₂ ≥ 60 AND ≥ 20 above baseline).
  • Monitor closely for hemodynamic instability during test.
  • Coordinate with ECMO team; document approach.
  • If apnea test cannot be safely completed → cerebral perfusion scintigraphy or CTA as ancillary.
  • Reference: Heard CMB & Fletcher JE (Pediatr Crit Care Med 2018).

A7. A 7-year-old post-arrest demonstrates triple flexion of the legs to noxious stimulation during exam. Does this exclude brain death?

Show answer
  • No — triple flexion is a spinal-mediated reflex.
  • Spinal reflexes do NOT exclude brain death.
  • Other spinal-mediated movements include deep tendon reflexes, plantar response, and rarely the Lazarus sign (complex arm flexion).
  • These movements originate in the spinal cord, not the brain.
  • Family education is critical to prevent distress.
  • Continue determination if other criteria met.

A8. A 5-year-old with severe facial trauma cannot have pupillary or corneal exam reliably performed. Discuss approach to brain death determination.

Show answer
  • Inability to complete relevant exam components is an indication for ancillary studies.
  • Document inability and rationale on checklist.
  • Continue with remaining exam components that can be performed.
  • Request ancillary study to confirm absence of cerebral blood flow:
    • Radionuclide cerebral perfusion scintigraphy ("hollow skull").
    • CT angiography (CTA) showing absence of intracranial filling.
    • Conventional cerebral angiography (gold standard).
  • Ancillary studies are NOT substitutes for clinical exam — they support determination when exam cannot be fully completed.
  • Document checklist; engage ethics if needed.

A9. A 14-year-old post-cardiac arrest in TTM at 33°C, day 4. Family is asking about brain death determination. Discuss timing.

Show answer
  • Defer determination — patient must be rewarmed to core temp ≥ 36°C first.
  • Hypothermia profoundly affects neurologic exam and apnea test.
  • Sedative half-lives prolonged during and after hypothermia — typically wait 24–72 h after rewarming before exam.
  • Verify sedatives metabolized (drug levels) and TOF 4/4 if paralytics used.
  • Once prerequisites met, proceed with standard two-examination protocol with 12 h interval (age > 30 d).
  • Communicate process and timeline to family with palliative care support.

A10. After completing brain death determination, when and how should organ donation be discussed?

Show answer
  • Donation discussion occurs AFTER brain death determination is complete.
  • Conducted by Organ Donation Organization (ODO) — separate from the determination team.
  • Separation reduces conflict of interest and maintains family trust.
  • Family makes the decision; cultural and religious considerations respected.
  • DBD pathway = Donation after Brain Death (when criteria met).
  • DCD pathway = Donation after Circulatory Death (when WLST elected without meeting brain death criteria).
  • Engage palliative care, social work, chaplaincy throughout.
  • Hormonal management for organ viability if donation proceeds (DDAVP, T3/T4, hydrocortisone, vasopressors).

Block B — Mock RCPSC Oral Cases (5 sequential, 40 minutes)

B1. A 9-year-old male was the unrestrained passenger in a high-speed MVC 8 days ago. Severe TBI; GCS 3 on arrival; ICP refractory; on pentobarbital coma until day 5; rewarmed; now off paralytics for 48 h, off sedation for 36 h. Family meeting today; the question of brain death determination has been raised.

B1a — Address prerequisites before initiating determination.
  • Verify cause: severe TBI is established irreversible cause.
  • Core temperature: confirm ≥ 36°C with temperature probe.
  • MAP: ensure age-appropriate (≥ 5th centile); reduce or stabilize vasopressors.
  • Sedatives: pentobarbital has long half-life; check serum level (or wait 4–5 half-lives — could be days).
  • Paralytics: confirm TOF 4/4 on peripheral nerve stimulator.
  • Metabolic: verify glucose 3.3–10, normal Na, K, Ca, Mg, P, normal renal/hepatic function.
  • Observation period: ≥ 24 h post-injury satisfied (day 8).
  • Document checklist.
B1b — Pentobarbital level returns at 0.8 µg/mL (well below therapeutic). Proceed with first examination.
  • Examination 1 by qualified physician:
    • Confirm coma — no response to deep noxious stimuli (supraorbital pressure, nail bed pressure, trapezius pinch).
    • Pupillary: midposition or dilated, unreactive (bright light in dark room).
    • Corneal: no blink to cotton wisp or saline.
    • Oculocephalic: avoid if c-spine status uncertain — verify c-spine cleared first.
    • Oculovestibular (cold caloric): HOB 30°, intact TM, 50 mL ice water each ear, ≥ 1 min observation, ≥ 5 min between.
    • Gag: deep posterior pharynx stimulation.
    • Cough: deep endotracheal suctioning.
    • Facial / motor: no purposeful response to central or peripheral noxious stimuli.
    • Spinal reflexes acceptable (note for family).
  • Apnea test:
    • Pre-oxygenate FiO₂ 1.0 × 10 min.
    • Baseline ABG (target PaCO₂ 35–45).
    • Disconnect ventilator; passive 100% O₂; observe 8–10 min.
    • Repeat ABG; check threshold PaCO₂ ≥ 60 AND ≥ 20 above baseline.
    • Test positive if no respiratory effort observed.
  • Document all findings on checklist with time stamps.
B1c — First examination consistent with brain death. Plan second examination.
  • Wait observation interval: 12 hours for age > 30 days.
  • Different qualified physician for second examination.
  • Verify prerequisites maintained between exams.
  • Full clinical exam + apnea test repeated.
  • If consistent → declare time of death (time of second positive apnea test).
  • Document carefully.
B1d — Determination complete. Discuss organ donation pathway with family.
  • ODO leads donation discussion, separately from determination team.
  • Approach family compassionately; allow time and space.
  • Cultural and religious considerations respected.
  • If family consents → maintain organ viability:
    • Hemodynamic support (vasopressors).
    • Hormonal replacement (DDAVP for DI, T3/T4 for hypothyroidism, hydrocortisone).
    • Lung-protective ventilation; oxygenation per protocol.
    • Antibiotics if infection.
  • Coordinate with transplant teams for OR timing.
  • UNOS donor management protocols.
B1e — Family declines donation. How to proceed.
  • Respect family decision.
  • Continue with appropriate notification (coroner, medical examiner if forensic case).
  • Per institutional protocol, mechanical support may be discontinued.
  • Bereavement support, social work, chaplaincy.
  • Allow family time for goodbye, rituals, presence.
  • Document all decisions and timeline.

B2. A 3-week-old neonate with severe perinatal HIE; received therapeutic hypothermia at 33.5°C × 72 h, now rewarmed. Day 8 post-birth. No clinical improvement. Family wants to understand prognosis and process.

B2a — Determination approach in this age group.
  • Term newborn (≥ 37 weeks) to 30 days: 24-hour observation interval.
  • Two complete examinations by different qualified physicians required.
  • Test sucking and rooting reflexes in addition to standard reflexes.
  • Verify rewarming (core temp ≥ 36°C) and sedative clearance.
  • Apnea test challenges: small lung volume, surfactant status, hemodynamic fragility.
  • Ancillary study (cerebral perfusion scintigraphy) preferred over EEG in this age due to inherently low-voltage neonatal background.
B2b — Perform first examination. Discuss findings expected.
  • Coma — no response to noxious.
  • Pupillary: midposition or dilated, unreactive.
  • Corneal: absent.
  • Oculocephalic/oculovestibular: absent.
  • Gag and cough: absent.
  • Sucking and rooting: absent (specifically tested in neonates).
  • Apnea test: PaCO₂ rise to ≥ 60 AND ≥ 20 above baseline with no respiratory effort.
  • Hypotension during apnea test is common in neonates — abort if needed.
B2c — Family requests information about what comes next.
  • Compassionate, honest communication.
  • Explain brain death determination process and rationale for 24-h observation interval.
  • Palliative care, social work, chaplaincy throughout.
  • Cultural and religious considerations respected.
  • Bereavement support; allow time for family presence and rituals.
  • Discuss next steps (second examination, then determination of death).
B2d — Apnea test aborted on second exam due to severe hypoxemia. Plan?
  • Document inability to complete apnea test with reason.
  • Request ancillary study — cerebral perfusion scintigraphy preferred in neonates.
  • EEG less reliable in neonates (low background voltage).
  • If ancillary study consistent with absence of cerebral blood flow → supports determination.
  • Document complete checklist with all components.
  • Multidisciplinary team coordination.

B3. A 6-year-old after near-drowning, post-cardiac arrest with 25 min CPR. Day 5 in PICU; received TTM at 33°C; rewarmed yesterday. Family has been asking about prognosis.

B3a — When can determination begin?
  • Defer until core temp ≥ 36°C (already done).
  • Wait additional 24–72 h post-rewarming for sedative metabolization.
  • Check serum drug levels if available.
  • Verify TOF 4/4.
  • Confirm hemodynamic stability and metabolic correction.
  • Total observation period typically ≥ 5–7 days post-arrest before determination.
B3b — Day 7, all prerequisites met. Plan determination.
  • Examination 1 with full clinical exam + apnea test.
  • Document each component on checklist.
  • Wait 12-h observation interval (age > 30 d).
  • Examination 2 by different qualified physician.
  • If consistent → declare time of death (time of second positive apnea test).
B3c — During first apnea test, SpO₂ drops to 78%. Manage.
  • Abort apnea test — SpO₂ < 85% sustained is mandatory abort criterion.
  • Reconnect ventilator.
  • Stabilize patient.
  • Request ancillary study (cerebral perfusion scintigraphy or CTA).
  • Document apnea test abort with reason and ABG values.
  • Continue with remaining clinical exam components if not yet done.
B3d — Family struggles to accept determination. Engage support.
  • Compassionate, honest communication.
  • Engage palliative care, ethics consult, chaplaincy.
  • Acknowledge cultural and religious considerations.
  • Allow time for family acceptance and rituals.
  • Provide written materials about brain death.
  • Follow institutional and provincial policy on body management if family persistently refuses.
  • Hospital legal counsel if needed.

B4. An 11-year-old after severe AHT (abusive head trauma) day 6. Complete diagnostic and forensic workup done; CPS involved. Suspected to meet brain death criteria.

B4a — Forensic considerations for brain death in AHT context.
  • Brain death determination follows same medical process.
  • Forensic implications: notify medical examiner / coroner.
  • Document all findings carefully — chain of custody considerations.
  • Coordinate with forensic pediatrics, CPS, and legal teams.
  • Autopsy may be required by law in suspected child abuse cases.
  • Continue safeguarding involvement throughout.
B4b — Perform determination.
  • Verify prerequisites (cause established, temp, MAP, drugs metabolized).
  • Examination 1: full clinical exam + apnea test.
  • Wait 12-h observation interval.
  • Examination 2 by different qualified physician.
  • Document all findings on standardized checklist.
  • Engage ODO separately after determination complete.
B4c — Family declines organ donation given criminal proceedings.
  • Respect family decision regardless of context.
  • ODO discussion remains separate from determination team and criminal investigation.
  • Coordinate with medical examiner — autopsy will proceed.
  • Continue safeguarding involvement (other children at home).
  • Multidisciplinary team meeting; honest documentation.
B4d — Long-term coordination after determination.
  • Medical examiner / coroner notification and autopsy.
  • CPS continues investigation for siblings and family.
  • Forensic pediatrics for medico-legal documentation.
  • Hospital legal counsel for testimony preparation.
  • Bereavement support for family.
  • Multidisciplinary team debriefing — these cases are emotionally heavy.

B5. A 10-year-old after severe TBI on VA-ECMO for refractory hemodynamics. Day 10. Cerebral function appears absent on examination. Family asking about brain death determination.

B5a — Challenges in ECMO patient.
  • VA-ECMO provides oxygenation and CO₂ removal — standard apnea test cannot be reliably performed.
  • Verify prerequisites (cause, temp, sedatives, drugs).
  • Modification: reduce sweep gas flow during test to allow CO₂ accumulation.
  • Serial ABGs to track PaCO₂.
  • Same threshold (PaCO₂ ≥ 60 AND ≥ 20 above baseline).
  • Monitor hemodynamic stability throughout.
  • Coordinate with ECMO team.
B5b — Apnea test on ECMO unable to achieve target PaCO₂ safely.
  • Abort test; document.
  • Request ancillary study — cerebral perfusion scintigraphy or CTA preferred for ECMO patients.
  • EEG possible but cerebral flow studies more definitive.
  • Continue with remaining clinical exam components.
  • Multidisciplinary determination team coordination.
B5c — Perfusion scintigraphy shows "hollow skull" — consistent with brain death. Plan second examination.
  • Wait 12-h observation interval.
  • Second examination by different qualified physician.
  • Repeat clinical exam + ancillary study (or repeat apnea test if feasible).
  • If consistent → declare time of death.
  • Document checklist carefully.
B5d — Plan ECMO discontinuation after determination.
  • After brain death declared, ECMO is providing somatic support, not therapy.
  • If donation pathway elected → continue ECMO until OR.
  • If no donation → discontinue ECMO per institutional protocol.
  • Family presence, comfort care, bereavement support.
  • Document time of cessation; coordinate with ODO if applicable.

Block C — Randomized SAQ Drill (30 questions)

C1. Define brain death.

Show answer
  • Irreversible cessation of all functions of the entire brain, including the brainstem.
  • Per UDDA 1981.

C2. Number of examinations required in pediatric brain death determination.

Show answer
  • Two complete examinations by different qualified physicians.
  • Each must include full clinical exam + apnea test (or ancillary if needed).

C3. Pediatric observation intervals (Nakagawa 2011).

Show answer
  • Term newborn to 30 days: 24 hours.
  • > 30 days to 18 years: 12 hours.

C4. Apnea test threshold.

Show answer
  • PaCO₂ ≥ 60 mm Hg AND ≥ 20 mm Hg above pre-test baseline.

C5. Apnea test abort criteria.

Show answer
  • SpO₂ < 85% sustained.
  • Significant hemodynamic instability.
  • Arrhythmia.
  • PaCO₂ target unreachable safely.

C6. Pre-oxygenation for apnea test.

Show answer
  • FiO₂ 1.0 for 10 minutes.
  • Target SpO₂ ~100%, PaO₂ ≥ 200 mm Hg.
  • Baseline ABG to establish PaCO₂.

C7. Brainstem reflexes that must be absent.

Show answer
  • Pupillary, corneal, oculocephalic/oculovestibular, gag, cough, sucking/rooting (neonates), respiratory effort.

C8. Spinal reflexes and brain death.

Show answer
  • Spinal reflexes (triple flexion, DTRs, plantar, Lazarus sign) do NOT exclude brain death.
  • Mediated by spinal cord, not brain.
  • Family education important.

C9. Prerequisites checklist.

Show answer
  • Known irreversible cause; temp ≥ 36°C; MAP age-appropriate; metabolic corrected; sedatives metabolized; NMB reversed (TOF 4/4); adequate observation period.

C10. Indications for ancillary studies.

Show answer
  • Clinical exam component cannot be completed.
  • Apnea test contraindicated or aborted.
  • Persistent confounders.
  • Concern about validity.
  • Shorten observation interval (Greer 2023).

C11. Types of ancillary studies.

Show answer
  • Cerebral perfusion scintigraphy (radionuclide; "hollow skull").
  • CT angiography.
  • Conventional angiography (gold standard).
  • EEG (electrocerebral silence per ACNS).
  • TCD (supportive, not stand-alone).

C12. Why is EEG less reliable in young infants?

Show answer
  • Background EEG may be inherently low voltage in neonates.
  • Falsely meeting "electrocerebral silence" criteria.
  • Perfusion studies generally preferred in this age.

C13. Apnea test on VA-ECMO.

Show answer
  • Reduce sweep gas flow during test.
  • Serial ABGs.
  • Same threshold (PaCO₂ ≥ 60 AND ≥ 20 above baseline).
  • If unable, use cerebral perfusion scintigraphy or CTA.

C14. Preterm (< 37 wk) brain death determination.

Show answer
  • Nakagawa 2011 does NOT provide formal criteria.
  • Case-by-case with neurology and neonatology input.
  • Cerebral perfusion studies often used.

C15. Cold caloric (oculovestibular) test technique.

Show answer
  • HOB 30°.
  • Confirm intact tympanic membrane.
  • 50 mL ice water in each ear.
  • Observe ≥ 1 min.
  • ≥ 5 min between ears.

C16. Cough reflex testing.

Show answer
  • Deep endotracheal suctioning to carina.
  • Observe for absence of cough or motor response.

C17. Pupillary findings in brain death.

Show answer
  • Midposition (4–6 mm) or dilated.
  • Unreactive to bright light.
  • Examined in dark room.

C18. Therapeutic hypothermia and brain death determination timing.

Show answer
  • Defer until rewarmed to ≥ 36°C.
  • Wait additional 24–72 h for sedative metabolization.
  • Verify TOF 4/4 if paralytics used.

C19. Lazarus sign.

Show answer
  • Complex spinal-mediated movement; rare.
  • Includes arm flexion, raising.
  • Does NOT exclude brain death.

C20. UDDA legal framework.

Show answer
  • Uniform Determination of Death Act (1981).
  • Death = irreversible cessation of (a) circulatory/respiratory function OR (b) all brain functions including brainstem.

C21. Greer 2023 vs Nakagawa 2011 distinctions.

Show answer
  • Greer 2023 unified pediatric and adult guidance.
  • Pediatric retains 2-exam standard with same intervals.
  • Adult guideline now allows ONE exam in many circumstances.
  • Refinements to ancillary use, ECMO testing, documentation.

C22. DBD vs DCD donation pathways.

Show answer
  • DBD: Donation after Brain Death — meets brain death criteria.
  • DCD: Donation after Circulatory Death — does not meet brain death criteria but family elects WLST.
  • Both family decision; ODO involvement.

C23. Time of death documentation.

Show answer
  • Time of second positive apnea test.
  • Or completion of ancillary study if used in second exam.

C24. Brain death vs persistent vegetative state.

Show answer
  • Brain death: irreversible cessation of all brain function (no awareness, no brainstem reflexes, no respiratory drive).
  • PVS: preserved brainstem function (breathing, reflexes) without cortical awareness.
  • PVS is not death.

C25. Locked-in syndrome.

Show answer
  • Preserved consciousness with paralysis (typically pontine lesion).
  • Vertical eye movements and blinking preserved.
  • NOT brain death.
  • Communication via blinking/eye movements.

C26. Cyanotic CHD and apnea test.

Show answer
  • Baseline SpO₂ may be lower than 85%.
  • Standard abort threshold may not apply.
  • Modify with cardiology input.
  • Ancillary often required.

C27. Severe pulmonary disease and apnea test.

Show answer
  • Baseline PaCO₂ may be elevated.
  • Apply ≥ 20 mm Hg above baseline.
  • If unable to safely achieve, ancillary study indicated.

C28. Organ donation conversation timing.

Show answer
  • AFTER brain death determination complete.
  • ODO leads, separate from determination team.
  • Reduces conflict of interest.

C29. Hormonal management for organ donation.

Show answer
  • DDAVP for central DI.
  • T3/T4 for hypothyroidism.
  • Hydrocortisone for adrenal insufficiency.
  • Vasopressor support; lung-protective ventilation.

C30. Common pediatric brain death causes.

Show answer
  • Severe TBI, AHT, anoxic-ischemic (drowning, cardiac arrest), intracranial hemorrhage, CNS infection, tumor with herniation.
