ZIMMERMAN Ch 59 — Neurosurgical Conditions — EXAM MODE

SOURCE: ZIMMERMAN   Companion to: ZIMMERMAN_59 STUDY MODE (Hydrocephalus, Chiari, Dandy-Walker, NTDs)

Block A — Mock RCPSC SAQ (10 questions, 12 min)

A1. Distinguish noncommunicating from communicating hydrocephalus with examples.

Show answer
  • Noncommunicating (obstructive): obstruction within ventricular system. Examples: aqueductal stenosis, colloid cyst, IVH-related obstruction, intraventricular tumor.
  • Communicating: obstruction at subarachnoid space, arachnoid granulations, or venous outflow. Examples: post-meningitis, post-SAH, post-IVH (late phase), venous sinus thrombosis.
  • Both can raise ICP — terminology refers only to anatomic site of obstruction.

A2. Outline ETV vs VP shunt indications and the ETV + CPC concept in infants.

Show answer
  • ETV (Endoscopic Third Ventriculostomy): creates communication between 3rd ventricle and prepontine cistern. Preferred for obstructive hydrocephalus, especially aqueductal stenosis.
  • VP shunt: for communicating hydrocephalus or when ETV not feasible.
  • ETV + CPC (choroid plexus cauterization) — Warf 2005 — superior to ETV alone in infants < 1 year. Reduces CSF production.
  • ETV Success Score predicts probability based on age, etiology, prior shunt.
  • Avoids lifelong shunt dependency and ~40% shunt failure rate within 2 years.

A3. A 6-month-old myelomeningocele patient post-closure now develops stridor and apnea. Explain.

Show answer
  • Chiari II decompensation — brainstem compression in small posterior fossa.
  • Vocal cord paralysis, swallowing dysfunction, apneic events, opisthotonos.
  • Treat hydrocephalus first (place or revise VP shunt) — often resolves brainstem symptoms.
  • If symptoms persist: posterior fossa decompression by neurosurgery.
  • Can be fatal — urgent neurosurgical evaluation.
  • ICU monitoring; airway management.

A4. List 4 prerequisites for the MOMS trial fetal myelomeningocele repair and the key outcomes.

Show answer
  • Singleton pregnancy.
  • Gestational age 19+0 to 25+6 weeks.
  • Lesion upper boundary T1–S1.
  • No other anomalies; maternal eligibility.
  • Outcomes (Adzick NEJM 2011): reduced need for VP shunt (40% vs 82%); improved motor function at 30 months.
  • Maternal risks: preterm labor, uterine dehiscence, premature rupture of membranes.

A5. A 10-year-old with occipital headache worse with cough and Valsalva. MRI: cerebellar tonsils 8 mm below foramen magnum. Diagnose and discuss management.

Show answer
  • Symptomatic Chiari I malformation.
  • Obtain spinal MRI to rule out syringomyelia (15–80% association).
  • Posterior fossa decompression: suboccipital craniectomy + C1 laminectomy ± duraplasty ± tonsillar reduction.
  • Outcomes: pain often improves; syrinx may resolve; scoliosis may stabilize.
  • Post-op risks: CSF leak, meningitis, hydrocephalus, pseudomeningocele.

A6. A neonate with lumbosacral sac in delivery room — manage.

Show answer
  • Protect sac with sterile, moist saline dressing.
  • Prone or side-lying position; avoid direct pressure on sac.
  • Latex precautions (lifelong — high allergy risk).
  • IV antibiotics (ampicillin + cefotaxime or per protocol).
  • Surgical closure by neurosurgery within 24–48 hours.
  • Monitor for hydrocephalus development (50–90% require VP shunt).
  • Watch for Chiari II decompensation (stridor, apnea, swallowing).

A7. List 4 typical features of Dandy-Walker malformation and 3 commonly associated systemic anomalies.

Show answer
  • Features:
    • Posterior fossa cyst continuous with 4th ventricle.
    • Cerebellar vermis hypoplasia or agenesis.
    • Elevated tentorium (high torcula).
    • Hydrocephalus in ~80%.
  • Associated systemic anomalies:
    • Congenital heart disease.
    • Cleft lip/palate.
    • Renal anomalies; genitourinary anomalies.
    • Trisomy 13, 18; PHACE syndrome; Joubert; Walker-Warburg.

A8. Outline 5 long-term complications of myelomeningocele.

Show answer
  • Hydrocephalus (50–90% require VP shunt).
  • Chiari II decompensation.
  • Neurogenic bladder (clean intermittent catheterization, urodynamics, anticholinergics).
  • Neurogenic bowel (bowel program).
  • Orthopedic deformities (clubfoot, scoliosis, hip dislocation).
  • Tethered cord (late symptoms requiring surgical detethering).
  • Skin breakdown from sensory loss.
  • Latex allergy/anaphylaxis lifelong.

A9. A child with VP shunt presents with vomiting, headache, sleepiness. Discuss workup.

Show answer
  • Shunt malfunction or infection.
  • Shunt series (skull, neck, chest, abdomen X-rays) for disconnection or migration.
  • Head CT for ventricular size and slit-vent assessment.
  • Neurosurgery consult for shunt tap.
  • CSF analysis: cell count, Gram stain, culture, glucose, protein, lactate.
  • If signs of infection: vancomycin + ceftazidime/cefepime/meropenem.
  • Likely revision; externalize if infection confirmed.

A10. Folic acid for NTD prevention — dose, mechanism, evidence.

Show answer
  • 400 µg/day preconception in all women planning pregnancy.
  • 4 mg/day if prior NTD pregnancy.
  • Mechanism: folate critical for nucleic acid synthesis during neural tube closure (4 weeks gestation).
  • ~70% reduction in NTD incidence (MRC Vitamin Study 1991).
  • Public health: dietary fortification has reduced NTDs substantially.

Block B — Oral Cases (5 sequential, 35 min)

B1. 2-month-old with rapidly enlarging head, sundowning, full fontanelle, irritability.

B1a — Initial assessment.
  • Acute hydrocephalus — surgical emergency.
  • Urgent ultrasound (open fontanelle) or CT.
  • ABCs; assess GCS; pupils.
  • Neurosurgery consult immediately.
  • Investigate cause: congenital aqueductal stenosis, post-IVH, infection, tumor.
B1b — Decompensating with worsening lethargy.
  • Hyperosmolar bolus (3% NaCl 3–5 mL/kg) as bridge.
  • HOB 30°; intubate if GCS ≤ 8.
  • Emergent EVD placement.
  • Continuous monitoring.
B1c — MRI shows aqueductal stenosis as cause.
  • Obstructive (noncommunicating) hydrocephalus.
  • Consider ETV — may avoid lifelong shunt.
  • ETV Success Score calculation.
  • If < 1 year, consider ETV + CPC (Warf protocol).
  • VP shunt as alternative.
B1d — Discharge planning.
  • Neurodevelopmental follow-up.
  • Annual MRI surveillance.
  • Family education on shunt malfunction warning signs.
  • Plan multidisciplinary care.

B2. Term newborn with lumbosacral myelomeningocele.

B2a — Delivery room management.
  • Protect sac with sterile, moist saline dressing.
  • Prone or side position.
  • Latex precautions implemented immediately.
  • IV antibiotics (ampicillin + cefotaxime).
  • NICU admission; neurosurgery consult.
  • Imaging: brain and spine MRI to assess Chiari II and define lesion level.
B2b — Day 1 — surgical closure plans.
  • Closure within 24–48 hours to prevent infection and ongoing neural damage.
  • Neurosurgery for closure; reconstruction by plastic surgery if needed.
  • Continue antibiotics perioperatively.
  • Monitor for CSF leak post-op.
B2c — Day 7 — head circumference increasing rapidly.
  • Developing hydrocephalus (50–90% incidence in MMC).
  • Ultrasound for ventricular size; head CT if needed.
  • Neurosurgery for VP shunt placement.
  • Monitor for Chiari II decompensation.
B2d — Long-term multidisciplinary plan.
  • Urology — neurogenic bladder management (CIC, anticholinergics, urodynamics).
  • Orthopedics — clubfoot, hip dysplasia, scoliosis.
  • PT/OT/SLP — mobility, ADLs, swallowing.
  • Neurodevelopmental pediatrics — cognitive assessment.
  • Bowel program.
  • Latex-free environment lifelong.
  • Family training and resources.

B3. 9-year-old with chronic morning headache, vomiting, and recent diplopia. MRI shows aqueductal stenosis.

B3a — Diagnosis and management approach.
  • Obstructive hydrocephalus from aqueductal stenosis.
  • Symptoms reflect raised ICP.
  • Neurosurgery for ETV (preferred over shunt at this age).
  • Calculate ETV Success Score.
B3b — Pre-operative considerations.
  • Hyperosmolar therapy on standby if decompensating.
  • HOB 30°.
  • Type and screen.
  • Pre-op imaging review with anatomic detail.
B3c — Post-operative complications to watch for.
  • CSF leak, infection.
  • Basilar artery injury (rare but serious during ETV).
  • Hypothalamic injury (memory, endocrine).
  • Inadequate flow → continued symptoms; may need shunt.
  • Subdural hygroma if rapid CSF decrease.
B3d — Family follow-up education.
  • Warning signs of obstruction recurrence.
  • Annual MRI to assess ventricle size and patency.
  • School integration plan.
  • Activity restrictions early post-op.

B4. 12-year-old with Chiari I diagnosed incidentally, asymptomatic. Family asks if surgery needed.

B4a — Approach to asymptomatic Chiari I.
  • Observation generally appropriate.
  • Obtain baseline spinal MRI to assess for syringomyelia.
  • Counsel family on warning signs: occipital headache, neck pain, sensory changes, weakness, scoliosis progression.
  • Serial clinical evaluation; repeat MRI per neurosurgery preference.
  • Surgical decompression reserved for symptomatic patients.
B4b — One year later develops cough headaches and arm weakness. MRI shows new syrinx.
  • Now symptomatic — surgical decompression indicated.
  • Posterior fossa decompression with possible duraplasty.
  • Syrinx may resolve post-decompression.
  • Pre-op anesthesia consult.
B4c — Post-op patient develops pseudomeningocele.
  • Common post-op complication.
  • Observation if asymptomatic and small.
  • Lumbar drain if large or persistent.
  • Surgical revision if persistent.
  • Rule out hydrocephalus.
B4d — Long-term follow-up.
  • Serial MRI to assess syrinx resolution.
  • Scoliosis monitoring (orthopedics).
  • Annual neurosurgery follow-up.

B5. 3-year-old with Dandy-Walker malformation, increasing head circumference.

B5a — Assessment and decision.
  • Hydrocephalus from cyst expansion.
  • MRI to define cyst-ventricle communication.
  • Neurosurgery: VP shunt, cyst-peritoneal shunt, or both.
  • ETV may be considered in selected cases.
B5b — Family asks about systemic concerns.
  • Screen for associated anomalies:
    • Echocardiogram for CHD.
    • Renal ultrasound.
    • Skeletal survey if other concerns.
  • Genetic consult — trisomy 13/18, PHACE, Joubert, Walker-Warburg.
  • Developmental assessment.
B5c — Discuss expected developmental outcomes.
  • Developmental delay common.
  • Outcomes depend on extent of brain malformation and associated anomalies.
  • Early Intervention referral.
  • Bayley assessments serially.
B5d — Long-term plan.
  • Multidisciplinary follow-up (neurology, neurosurgery, cardiology, genetics, developmental pediatrics).
  • Annual MRI.
  • School support plan.
  • Family resources.

Block C — Randomized SAQ Drill (20)

C1. Monro-Kellie doctrine.

Show
  • Skull is fixed; brain + blood + CSF in equilibrium; increase in one needs compensation in another, else ICP rises.

C2. Open fontanelle and herniation.

Show
  • Open fontanelle delays, but does NOT prevent, herniation once compensation exhausted.

C3. VP shunt failure rate.

Show
  • ~40% within 2 years; multiple revisions common over lifetime.

C4. ETV success score components.

Show
  • Age, etiology of hydrocephalus, history of prior shunt.

C5. Chiari I MRI diagnostic criterion.

Show
  • Cerebellar tonsils > 5 mm below foramen magnum; pointed tonsils more pathologic.

C6. Chiari II key association.

Show
  • Myelomeningocele essentially all cases; hydrocephalus nearly universal.

C7. Chiari II brainstem warning signs.

Show
  • Stridor (vocal cord paralysis), apnea, swallowing difficulty, opisthotonos.

C8. Syringomyelia in Chiari I incidence.

Show
  • 15–80%; always obtain spinal MRI.

C9. Dandy-Walker components.

Show
  • Posterior fossa cyst + vermis hypoplasia/agenesis + elevated tentorium; hydrocephalus in ~80%.

C10. MOMS trial outcomes.

Show
  • Reduced shunt need (40% vs 82%); improved motor function at 30 months.

C11. Folic acid for NTD prevention.

Show
  • 400 µg/d preconception; 4 mg/d if prior NTD pregnancy; 70% reduction.

C12. Encephalocele vs cranial meningocele.

Show
  • Encephalocele: brain + meninges; meningocele: meninges only (better prognosis).

C13. Arachnoid cyst surgical indication.

Show
  • Symptomatic mass effect, hemorrhage, growth, hydrocephalus; endoscopic fenestration preferred.

C14. Acute hydrocephalus hyperosmolar dose.

Show
  • 3% NaCl 3–5 mL/kg IV; mannitol 0.25–1 g/kg.

C15. VP shunt overdrainage features.

Show
  • Slit ventricles, postural headache, subdural hygroma/hematoma; treat with programmable valve adjustment.

C16. Myelomeningocele postnatal timeline.

Show
  • Protect sac → IV antibiotics → closure 24–48 h → monitor hydrocephalus → VP shunt as needed.

C17. Tethered cord symptoms.

Show
  • Back pain, leg weakness, sensory loss, urinary deterioration, foot deformity, scoliosis progression.

C18. Latex precautions in MMC patients.

Show
  • Lifelong latex-free environment; high anaphylaxis risk; EpiPen at home; allergy bracelet.

C19. Pott's puffy tumor.

Show
  • Subperiosteal abscess of frontal bone from frontal sinusitis with intracranial complications; surgical drainage + IV antibiotics.

C20. Aqueductal stenosis most common cause.

Show
  • Congenital aqueductal stenosis is most common cause of congenital obstructive hydrocephalus (~30%).

Block D — Randomized PICU Case Drill (15)

D1. 2-mo rapidly enlarging head, sundowning.

Show
  • Hydrocephalus emergency; US/CT; EVD; treat cause.

D2. Term newborn with sac in delivery room.

Show
  • Protect sac; latex-free; antibiotics; closure 24-48 h; monitor hydrocephalus.

D3. 9-yo morning HA + aqueductal stenosis.

Show
  • ETV; calculate ETV Success Score; postoperative monitoring.

D4. Premature 28-wk post-IVH hydrocephalus.

Show
  • Serial taps; VP shunt when stable weight > 2 kg and CSF clears; neurodev follow-up.

D5. 11-yo Chiari I + 8 mm tonsils + cough HA.

Show
  • Symptomatic; spinal MRI for syrinx; posterior fossa decompression.

D6. 6-mo MMC post-closure + new stridor.

Show
  • Chiari II decompensation; treat hydrocephalus first; PF decompression if persists; ICU monitor.

D7. 3-yo DWM with ↑HC.

Show
  • VP/cyst-peritoneal shunt or both; systemic anomaly screen; genetics; dev follow-up.

D8. Infant occipital encephalocele.

Show
  • Protect sac; imaging; neurosurgery repair; manage hydrocephalus; long-term neurodev.

D9. 8-yo seizure + L middle fossa arachnoid cyst.

Show
  • Symptomatic cyst; endoscopic fenestration; antiepileptic management.

D10. VP shunt patient with vomiting, lethargy.

Show
  • Shunt series; head CT; neurosurgery consult; likely revision.

D11. 2-wk post-MMC closure rapid HC growth.

Show
  • Developing hydrocephalus; VP shunt; monitor for complications; multidisciplinary team.

D12. 12-yo Chiari I + syrinx + scoliosis.

Show
  • Posterior fossa decompression; orthopedics co-management; serial MRI to assess syrinx.

D13. Adolescent with MMC + new urinary symptoms + back pain.

Show
  • Tethered cord syndrome; MRI lumbar spine; surgical detethering; urodynamics.

D14. 10-yo MMC + latex anaphylaxis dental procedure.

Show
  • Epinephrine, fluids, antihistamines, steroids; latex precautions reinforced; allergy bracelet; EpiPen.

D15. Counseling pregnant woman with fetal MMC at 22 weeks.

Show
  • Fetal therapy team referral; MOMS criteria; benefits + maternal risks; folic acid for future pregnancies.
