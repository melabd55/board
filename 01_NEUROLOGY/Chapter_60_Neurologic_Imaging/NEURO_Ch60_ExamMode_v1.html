Mock RCPSC Block A — 10 SAQs (timed)

⏱ 30 min total · 3 min per SAQ
SAQ A-1
When is CT preferred over MRI in pediatric neurocritical care?
Show Answer
  • Acute trauma — fast detection of hemorrhage and fractures.
  • Suspected raised ICP / herniation.
  • Unstable patient.
  • Bony injury (skull, cervical spine).
  • MRI contraindicated (pacemaker, metal foreign body).
  • Before LP if focal signs / immunocompromised.
SAQ A-2
Why may CT be normal in hyperacute stroke?
Show Answer
  • Tissue density changes take hours to develop.
  • CT may show subtle insular ribbon loss or dense MCA sign within first hours.
  • Frank hypodensity usually requires 6–24 h.
  • MRI DWI detects cytotoxic edema within minutes.
SAQ A-3
Differentiate epidural vs. subdural hematoma on CT.
Show Answer
  • Epidural — biconvex (lens-shaped); does not cross sutures; arterial bleed (middle meningeal artery); often associated skull fracture; may have "lucid interval."
  • Subdural — crescent-shaped; crosses sutures but not midline; venous (bridging vein); common in abusive head trauma.
SAQ A-4
Why is SWI the key sequence in DAI?
Show Answer
  • SWI highly sensitive to magnetic susceptibility (deoxy-Hb, hemosiderin).
  • Detects microhemorrhages 2–6× more than GRE.
  • DAI distribution — gray-white junction, corpus callosum, brainstem.
  • Number, volume, location of lesions correlate with severity and outcome.
SAQ A-5
How does DWI distinguish abscess from necrotic tumor?
Show Answer
  • Abscess — central pus restricts diffusion (DWI bright, ADC dark).
  • Necrotic tumor — center does NOT restrict (DWI dark / T2 shine-through, ADC bright).
  • MRS — abscess shows amino acids / lactate / lipid; tumor shows choline peak.
SAQ A-6
Neonatal HIE imaging patterns.
Show Answer
  • Mild — normal MRI.
  • Moderate — watershed (parasagittal) pattern in older infants.
  • Severe — central pattern (BG, thalami, perirolandic cortex, posterior limb internal capsule).
  • Profound — global with brainstem.
  • Image at 4–7 days post-injury for best correlation.
SAQ A-7
PECARN head injury rule basics.
Show Answer
  • Identifies very low-risk children with minor head trauma.
  • Separate rules for <2 y and ≥2 y.
  • Predictors — GCS, mental status, scalp hematoma, LOC, mechanism, basilar fracture signs, vomiting (≥2 y), headache.
  • No predictors → CT not needed (sensitivity for clinically important TBI ~99 %).
SAQ A-8
"Empty delta sign" significance.
Show Answer
  • Triangular filling defect in superior sagittal sinus on contrast CT.
  • Indicates cerebral sinovenous thrombosis.
  • MRV is gold standard for confirmation.
  • Treat with anticoagulation per pediatric guidelines.
SAQ A-9
Pediatric MS imaging features.
Show Answer
  • Periventricular ovoid lesions perpendicular to ventricles (Dawson fingers).
  • Juxtacortical lesions.
  • Cervical cord short-segment lesions.
  • Some enhancement of acute lesions.
  • Diagnosis requires dissemination in space and time.
SAQ A-10
Imaging features of abusive head trauma.
Show Answer
  • Subdural hematomas of different ages.
  • Interhemispheric subdurals.
  • Retinal hemorrhages on fundoscopy.
  • Posterior rib / metaphyseal corner fractures on skeletal survey.
  • Hypoxic-ischemic injury pattern.
  • SWI shows microhemorrhages of different ages.

Mock RCPSC Block B — 5 Sequential ICU Cases

⏱ 30 min total · 6 min per case
CASE B-1
7-year-old with sudden right hemiparesis; CT head normal.
Q. Next imaging step?
Show Answer
  • MRI with DWI / MRA emergently — CT may be normal in first 24 h.
  • Consider tPA / thrombectomy per pediatric stroke protocols.
  • If sickle cell — exchange transfusion to HbS <30 % concurrently.
CASE B-2
5-month-old with seizures; CT shows interhemispheric subdural hematoma; retinal hemorrhages on fundoscopy.
Q. Imaging follow-up.
Show Answer
  • MRI with SWI 3–5 days after CT — DAI, microhemorrhages of different ages.
  • Skeletal survey for occult fractures.
  • Report to child protection.
  • Multidisciplinary investigation.
CASE B-3
9-year-old immunocompromised with fever and headache; MRI shows ring-enhancing right frontal lesion.
Q. Differential and key sequence.
Show Answer
  • DWI — central restriction → abscess; no restriction → necrotic tumor.
  • MRS — amino acids / lactate for abscess; choline for tumor.
  • Investigate organisms in immunocompromised (Toxoplasma, fungal).
  • Antibiotics + surgical drainage if abscess.
CASE B-4
Term newborn with HIE on hypothermia day 4.
Q. MRI plan.
Show Answer
  • MRI at 4–7 days post-injury — DWI peak.
  • Include MRS for lactate / NAA ratios.
  • Patterns — central (BG, thalamus, PLIC), watershed, or global — drive prognosis.
  • Family discussion of imaging findings.
CASE B-5
12-year-old with mastoiditis develops focal seizures; MRV shows lack of flow in left transverse sinus.
Q. Diagnosis and management.
Show Answer
  • Cerebral sinovenous thrombosis (lateral sinus).
  • Treat mastoiditis — antibiotics, surgical drainage.
  • Anticoagulation (LMWH then warfarin/DOAC) for 3–6 months.
  • Treat seizures.
  • Follow-up MRV.

Block C — Remaining SAQs (randomized)

Q C-1
List five advantages of MRI over CT.
Show Answer
  • No ionizing radiation.
  • Greater anatomic and metabolic detail.
  • Better gray-white differentiation.
  • Better posterior fossa / brainstem visualization.
  • Multiple sequences — DWI, SWI, MRS, MRA/MRV.
  • Better spinal cord soft-tissue resolution.
Q C-2
Hounsfield unit values for key tissues.
Show Answer
  • Air −1000.
  • Fat −100.
  • CSF / water 0.
  • Brain 30–40.
  • Acute blood 50–80.
  • Calcification / bone +1000.
Q C-3
HSV encephalitis imaging features.
Show Answer
  • Temporal lobe / limbic system T2/FLAIR hyperintensity.
  • Often asymmetric — unilateral temporal involvement classic.
  • Hemorrhagic component on SWI.
  • Cingulate gyrus may also be involved.
  • EEG — PLEDs over temporal region.
Q C-4
Tuberculous meningitis imaging.
Show Answer
  • Basilar meningitis with thick enhancement.
  • Hydrocephalus.
  • Tuberculomas — ring-enhancing parenchymal lesions.
  • Infarctions — especially basal ganglia.
  • "TB triad" — basilar exudates + hydrocephalus + basal ganglia infarct.
Q C-5
NMOSD imaging features.
Show Answer
  • Longitudinally extensive transverse myelitis >3 vertebral segments.
  • Bilateral simultaneous optic neuritis.
  • Area postrema lesions.
  • Hypothalamic lesions.
  • Less commonly periventricular.
  • Anti-AQP4 antibody confirms.
Q C-6
Inborn error patterns — Leigh syndrome.
Show Answer
  • Mitochondrial disease.
  • Symmetric BG, brainstem, periaqueductal gray T2 hyperintensity.
  • MRS — elevated lactate.
  • Avoid valproate, prolonged propofol.
  • Levetiracetam first-line for seizures.
Q C-7
X-linked adrenoleukodystrophy imaging.
Show Answer
  • Bilateral posterior parieto-occipital demyelination.
  • Enhancing leading edge of demyelination.
  • Symmetric, progressive.
  • BMT if early; check VLCFA and ABCD1 gene.
Q C-8
Pediatric posterior fossa tumors.
Show Answer
  • Medulloblastoma — midline / vermis; restricts on DWI.
  • Pilocytic astrocytoma — cerebellar; cystic with enhancing mural nodule; low DWI.
  • Ependymoma — fourth ventricle; "plastic" extending through foramen of Magendie.
  • DIPG — diffuse pontine expansion; non-enhancing.
Q C-9
When to use gadolinium and when to avoid.
Show Answer
  • Use — infection, inflammation, tumor, vascular evaluation.
  • Avoid — eGFR <30 (NSF risk).
  • Trace brain / bone retention documented — use only when contrast adds diagnostic value.
  • Prefer macrocyclic over linear agents.
Q C-10
CT for cervical spine.
Show Answer
  • Bony detail — fractures, dislocations.
  • High-risk pediatric mechanisms — high-speed MVC, fall >3 m, axial load, focal neuro signs.
  • SCIWORA — normal CT does not exclude cord injury; MRI required.
Q C-11
ALARA principle.
Show Answer
  • As Low As Reasonably Achievable.
  • Minimize ionizing radiation dose.
  • Pediatric protocols — weight-based, low-kVp, iterative reconstruction.
  • Use MRI when feasible.
  • Avoid repeat scans without clear change.
Q C-12
SCIWORA features.
Show Answer
  • Spinal cord injury without radiographic abnormality.
  • Common in young children (<8 y) — elastic spine.
  • CT / X-ray normal; MRI shows cord edema, hemorrhage, contusion.
  • Mechanism — sudden flexion / extension.
  • Spinal immobilization, MRI, neurosurgical consultation.
Q C-13
Subarachnoid hemorrhage CT findings.
Show Answer
  • Sulcal hyperdensity.
  • Often along tentorium / interhemispheric fissure.
  • Pediatric SAH — typically AVM, aneurysm, trauma.
  • CT sensitivity ~95 % within 6 h; falls thereafter.
  • If CT negative and high suspicion, LP for xanthochromia.
Q C-14
Cytotoxic vs. vasogenic edema on MRI.
Show Answer
  • Cytotoxic — DWI bright, ADC dark; restricted diffusion; stroke.
  • Vasogenic — DWI dark / T2 shine-through, ADC bright; tumor / abscess / PRES.
  • Distinction guides differential and management.
Q C-15
Imaging in pediatric brain death evaluation.
Show Answer
  • Radionuclide CBF scan — no intracranial uptake.
  • CT angiography — no opacification of major intracranial arteries.
  • TCD — oscillating flow or systolic spikes only.
  • Used when clinical apnea test cannot be safely performed.
Q C-16
MRS clinical applications.
Show Answer
  • NAA — neuronal integrity (decreased in HIE, TBI).
  • Choline — membrane turnover (increased in tumor).
  • Creatine — energy reference; ratios reported.
  • Lactate peak — anaerobic metabolism.
  • Lipid / amino acids — abscess.
Q C-17
Imaging strategy in suspected encephalitis.
Show Answer
  • MRI with FLAIR, T2, DWI, post-gadolinium.
  • HSV — temporal lobe.
  • Autoimmune (NMDAR) — often normal early.
  • ADEM — multifocal large T2/FLAIR.
  • JE / enteroviral — thalamus, BG, brainstem.
Q C-18
Anti-NMDAR encephalitis imaging.
Show Answer
  • MRI often normal early.
  • Subtle limbic, hippocampal, cerebellar T2 hyperintensity in some.
  • EEG — extreme delta brush.
  • CSF antibody positivity drives diagnosis.
  • Search for tumor.
Q C-19
Cerebral abscess imaging.
Show Answer
  • Ring-enhancing lesion with central DWI restriction.
  • Surrounding vasogenic edema.
  • "Dual rim sign" on SWI.
  • MRS — amino acids, lactate, lipid.
  • Differential — necrotic tumor (no DWI restriction).
Q C-20
CSVT imaging diagnosis.
Show Answer
  • MRV — gold standard; lack of flow in affected sinus.
  • CT — "empty delta sign" in superior sagittal sinus.
  • Non-contrast CT — hyperdense sinus / cord sign.
  • SWI — venous engorgement, microhemorrhages.
  • Anticoagulation per pediatric guidelines.
Q C-21
PRES imaging features.
Show Answer
  • Bilateral parieto-occipital white matter T2/FLAIR hyperintensity.
  • Vasogenic edema (DWI dark, ADC bright).
  • May involve cerebellum, brainstem (atypical).
  • Causes — hypertension, immunosuppressants, eclampsia, chemo.
  • Usually reversible with cause control.
Q C-22
Hypoglycemic brain injury imaging.
Show Answer
  • Parieto-occipital cortex affected — selectively vulnerable.
  • DWI restriction in cortex and white matter.
  • BG / hippocampus also affected.
  • Posterior predominance distinguishes from HIE.
Q C-23
Cerebral venous infarct imaging.
Show Answer
  • Cortical / subcortical hemorrhage not in arterial distribution.
  • Often parasagittal (SSS thrombosis).
  • Vasogenic + cytotoxic edema (mixed DWI signal).
  • Treat underlying CSVT with anticoagulation.
Q C-24
Glutaric aciduria type 1 imaging.
Show Answer
  • Widened Sylvian fissures, frontotemporal atrophy ("bat-wing").
  • Subdural hematomas (mimic AHT).
  • Bilateral basal ganglia signal abnormality.
  • Macrocephaly common.
  • Distinguish from non-accidental trauma.
Q C-25
Imaging in hypothalamic / pituitary tumors.
Show Answer
  • Craniopharyngioma — suprasellar; calcifications on CT; cystic + solid on MRI; enhancement.
  • Optic pathway glioma — bilateral in NF1.
  • Germinoma — pineal / suprasellar; restricts on DWI.
  • Hypothalamic hamartoma — iso-intense to gray matter; gelastic seizures.
Q C-26
MRI safety considerations.
Show Answer
  • Pacemakers — MR-conditional newer models acceptable; otherwise contraindicated.
  • Cochlear implants — generally contraindicated.
  • Metallic foreign bodies — screen.
  • Aneurysm clips — confirm MR-compatibility.
  • Sedation risks; hearing protection mandatory.
Q C-27
Ultrasound use in PICU neonate.
Show Answer
  • Open fontanelle enables transcranial US up to ~6 months.
  • Detects IVH, hydrocephalus, PVL.
  • Daily US on ECMO for stroke / hemorrhage.
  • Bedside, no sedation, no radiation.
  • Limited for cortical and posterior fossa pathology.
Q C-28
CT for skull fractures, MRI for cord injury — why?
Show Answer
  • CT — superior bony detail (bone window).
  • MRI — superior soft-tissue / cord / ligament detail.
  • Both modalities complement each other in trauma.
  • CT cervical spine cleared, but persistent neuro signs → MRI cervical / brain.
Q C-29
Future directions in pediatric neuroimaging.
Show Answer
  • Faster MRI sequences (unsedated scans).
  • Machine learning for image interpretation.
  • Quantitative imaging biomarkers.
  • Functional MRI for cognitive mapping.
  • PET-MRI hybrid.
  • Photon-counting CT.
Q C-30
ADEM imaging features.
Show Answer
  • Multifocal large T2/FLAIR hyperintensities.
  • Involves white matter AND gray matter.
  • Thalamic / basal ganglia / cortical involvement common.
  • May enhance with gadolinium.
  • Differentiate from MS (Dawson fingers) and NMOSD (LETM).

Block D — Remaining Cases (randomized)

CASE D-1
10-year-old after falling off bike; lucid interval, then deterioration. CT — biconvex hyperdense collection.
Q. Diagnosis and management.
Show Answer
  • Epidural hematoma — biconvex, does not cross sutures.
  • Arterial bleed (middle meningeal artery).
  • Emergent neurosurgical evacuation if mass effect.
  • Volume / thickness thresholds variable in pediatrics.
CASE D-2
4-year-old with fever, seizures; MRI shows asymmetric right temporal lobe T2 hyperintensity with hemorrhagic SWI.
Q. Diagnosis and management.
Show Answer
  • HSV encephalitis.
  • CSF HSV PCR.
  • Empiric acyclovir 30 mg/kg/day IV.
  • 21-day course.
  • Continuous EEG; monitor for cerebral edema.
CASE D-3
14-year-old TBI; CT no overt hematoma but MRI SWI shows punctate microhemorrhages in corpus callosum and gray-white junction.
Q. Diagnosis.
Show Answer
  • Diffuse axonal injury (DAI).
  • SWI is the most sensitive sequence.
  • Severity correlates with location.
  • Prognostic — number, volume, location.
CASE D-4
15-year-old female with optic neuritis; MRI brain shows periventricular ovoid lesions perpendicular to ventricles.
Q. Likely diagnosis.
Show Answer
  • Pediatric multiple sclerosis (Dawson fingers).
  • Dissemination in space and time required for definite diagnosis.
  • Treat acute attack with high-dose steroids.
  • DMT decision with neurology.
CASE D-5
14-year-old with bilateral optic neuritis and LETM over 4 vertebral segments.
Q. Diagnosis.
Show Answer
  • NMOSD likely; anti-AQP4 confirmation.
  • Acute — IV methylprednisolone, PLEX.
  • Maintenance — rituximab, eculizumab, satralizumab.
  • Avoid β-interferons.
CASE D-6
7-year-old, 2 weeks post-URI, encephalopathy + multifocal deficits; MRI multifocal large T2/FLAIR involving WM and GM.
Q. Diagnosis and treatment.
Show Answer
  • ADEM.
  • High-dose IV methylprednisolone × 3–5 days.
  • IVIG / PLEX if refractory.
  • Anti-MOG antibody testing.
  • Most pediatric cases recover well.
CASE D-7
2-year-old with developmental regression and lactic acidosis; MRI symmetric T2 hyperintensity in BG and brainstem; MRS lactate peak.
Q. Diagnosis.
Show Answer
  • Leigh syndrome (mitochondrial).
  • Genetic confirmation.
  • Avoid valproate, prolonged propofol.
  • Levetiracetam first-line for seizures.
CASE D-8
8-year-old boy with cognitive regression and visual problems; MRI bilateral posterior parieto-occipital demyelination with enhancing edge.
Q. Diagnosis and management.
Show Answer
  • X-linked adrenoleukodystrophy.
  • VLCFA + ABCD1 gene.
  • BMT if early in disease.
  • Adrenal insufficiency screening.
  • Family screening.
CASE D-9
6-year-old with morning vomiting and ataxia; MRI cystic posterior fossa lesion with enhancing mural nodule.
Q. Likely tumor.
Show Answer
  • Pilocytic astrocytoma.
  • WHO grade 1.
  • Surgical resection often curative.
  • Manage hydrocephalus.
CASE D-10
5-year-old with morning headache and vomiting; MRI midline posterior fossa mass restricting on DWI.
Q. Diagnosis.
Show Answer
  • Medulloblastoma.
  • Maximal safe resection + RT + chemo.
  • Subtypes — WNT (best), SHH, group 3 (worst), group 4.
  • Avoid cranial RT in <3 y if possible.
CASE D-11
Adolescent immigrant with subacute headache, fever; MRI basilar meningitis, hydrocephalus, BG infarction.
Q. Diagnosis and management.
Show Answer
  • Tuberculous meningitis (TB triad).
  • LP — lymphocytic, low glucose, high protein, AFB smear/PCR.
  • 4-drug regimen + dexamethasone.
  • EVD / VP shunt for hydrocephalus.
CASE D-12
14-year-old post-renal transplant on tacrolimus with hypertension, seizures, cortical blindness; MRI bilateral parieto-occipital T2/FLAIR.
Q. Diagnosis.
Show Answer
  • PRES.
  • Control hypertension.
  • Reduce / stop tacrolimus.
  • Treat seizures.
  • Usually reversible.
CASE D-13
8-month-old macrocephaly + subdural; MRI wide Sylvian fissures, frontotemporal atrophy.
Q. Differential.
Show Answer
  • Glutaric aciduria type 1 — can mimic AHT.
  • Urine organic acids; acylcarnitine.
  • Genetic — GCDH.
  • Treat — lysine restriction, carnitine, riboflavin.
CASE D-14
15-year-old psychiatric symptoms, dyskinesias, seizures; MRI initially normal; CSF anti-NMDAR antibody positive.
Q. Imaging interpretation.
Show Answer
  • MRI often normal in anti-NMDAR encephalitis.
  • EEG — extreme delta brush.
  • Search for tumor (ovarian teratoma).
  • Steroids + IVIG/PLEX first-line; rituximab/cyclophosphamide refractory.
CASE D-15
10-year-old growth failure, visual changes, polyuria; MRI suprasellar mass cystic + solid; calcifications on CT.
Q. Diagnosis.
Show Answer
  • Craniopharyngioma.
  • Endocrine evaluation — hypopituitarism common.
  • Visual field testing.
  • Surgical ± RT.
  • Stress-dose steroids perioperatively; DI prophylaxis.
CASE D-16
3-month-old with severe hypoglycemia; MRI parieto-occipital cortical / subcortical DWI restriction.
Q. Interpretation.
Show Answer
  • Hypoglycemic brain injury — selective posterior vulnerability.
  • Distinguishes from HIE (BG / thalamus preferred).
  • Workup — hyperinsulinism, FAOD, gluconeogenesis defect.
CASE D-17
6-year-old new right hemiparesis; MRI left MCA infarct; MRA narrowed M1 with beading.
Q. Etiology.
Show Answer
  • Focal cerebral arteriopathy (FCA).
  • Post-VZV vasculitis frequently associated.
  • Antiplatelet therapy.
  • Steroids in some.
  • Monitor for progression.
CASE D-18
8-year-old with recurrent TIAs; MRA bilateral ICA narrowing with "puff of smoke" collaterals.
Q. Diagnosis and management.
Show Answer
  • Moyamoya disease (or syndrome).
  • Revascularization — direct or indirect.
  • Avoid hypotension, hyperventilation, dehydration.
  • Aspirin for secondary prevention.
CASE D-19
14-year-old after severe TBI; clinical brain death exam complete; CTA shows no opacification of intracranial arteries.
Q. Interpretation.
Show Answer
  • Supports brain death determination.
  • Confirmed ancillary test plus clinical exam.
  • Family communication and organ donation discussion.
CASE D-20
4-year-old ependymoma with hydrocephalus.
Q. Imaging finding and management.
Show Answer
  • Ependymoma — "plastic" tumor; fourth ventricle; extends through foramen of Magendie.
  • Surgical resection + radiation.
  • Manage hydrocephalus with EVD or VP shunt.
  • Often residual disease due to brainstem adherence.
